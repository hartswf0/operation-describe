## Patterns Draft F Highlights

### 1. The Argument Is a Chain, Not a Taxonomy
Draft F's deepest structural insight is that the paper's argument is an **escalating desire chain**: word → image → world. This chain appears in the final assembled draft as a three-threshold structure, but Draft F states it with more emotional and philosophical force. The chain is not merely chronological (ancient → contemporary → emergent). It is *appetitive*: "The word wanted the image. The image wanted the world."

### 2. Cultivation, Not Command
Draft F's greenhouse/seed/cultivation metaphor cluster is **entirely absent** from the final assembled draft, which uses more institutional language ("constitution," "governance," "constraints"). The greenhouse metaphor captures something the constitution metaphor misses: that prompting is *organic*, iterative, and never finished. Both metaphors should coexist.

### 3. Theology of the Natural Sign
Draft F makes a move the final draft only gestures toward: ekphrastic hope is **theological**. "Immediacy becomes inhabitation, and both promises are theological in disguise." This connects the paper to Bolter & Grusin's immediacy/hypermediacy and to the deeper history of the natural sign (Cratylus, Adamic language, etc.). The final draft should acknowledge this.

### 4. The Polemic
Draft F ends with a direct address to humanists: "Humanists must stop treating generative AI as a vulgar technical novelty or a degraded image factory. They must rebuild ekphrasis around prompting, process, platform realism, and worldtext." The final assembled draft is more restrained. Whether to include this depends on the target venue.
