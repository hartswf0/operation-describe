## Definition

**Abundance** is the condition in which AI generates not one image but a multiplicity. The same prompt produces variations; the latent space contains near-infinite alternatives. The ekphrast does not produce a work but **samples from a distribution**.

This challenges:
- **Artistic production**: the maker does not make; they select
- **Authorship**: if the output is a sample, not a creation, who is the author?
- **Uniqueness**: Benjamin's aura — the "here and now" of the original — dissolves when every prompt spawns a field of alternatives
- **The paragone**: the competition between word and image presupposed scarcity (one poem, one painting); abundance makes the competition meaningless

## The Double Reading

Abundance is the **same phenomenon** read two ways:

| Frame | Reading | Theorist |
|-------|---------|----------|
| **Affirmative** | Generative surplus = creative democratization, exploratory richness, the ekphrast as navigator of possibility | [[entity-watson-hartsoe]] |
| **Critical** | Mass overproduction = visual pollution, waste, self-consuming loop, homogenization toward the mean | [[entity-roland-meyer]] ([[concept-platform-realism]]) |

The tension between these two readings constitutes [[conflict-abundance-vs-platform-realism]] — the central aesthetic-political conflict of operative ekphrasis.

## Abundance and Benjamin

Benjamin's "The Work of Art in the Age of Mechanical Reproduction" (1935) argued that mechanical reproduction destroys the artwork's aura — its unique presence in time and space. AI abundance takes this further:

- Photography reproduced *existing* images → loss of aura through copying
- AI generates *non-existing* images → there was never an original to have aura
- The question shifts from "is this a faithful copy?" to "is this a meaningful sample?"

Abundance is not the loss of aura but the condition in which **aura was never possible** because the image was always one of an uncountable multiplicity.

## Abundance and Thick Prompting

[[thick-prompt]] provides the response to abundance's threat of meaninglessness. If every prompt spawns infinite alternatives, the thick prompt is the instrument that specifies which samples are *significant* — not through compositional uniqueness (impossible in abundance) but through **situated meaning** (Layers 2–6: native meaning, code, audience, stakes, reclassification).

A thin prompt in abundance produces interchangeable images. A thick prompt in abundance produces images that — though they could have been otherwise — carry irreplaceable significance because their context was built into the prompt.
