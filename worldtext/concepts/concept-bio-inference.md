## Definition

**Bio-inference** is the thesis that the human act of reading is structurally identical to a diffusion model's inference pass. The reader takes a sparse, low-dimensional textual signal (the book) and "denoises" it into a dense, high-dimensional experiential world (the mental image, the felt narrative) using the "weights" of their own accumulated life experience, prior reading, and cultural membership.

The novel is a **zero-shot prompt** for the human latent space.

## The Key Moves

### 1. Iser's Gaps = Noise

Wolfgang Iser's theory of textual indeterminacy says every text contains **gaps** — places where the reader must supply connections, fill absences, and produce meaning not explicitly given. These gaps are not failures of the text but constitutive features: they are what makes reading an *active* process rather than passive reception.

In the bio-inference model, Iser's gaps are isomorphic to the Gaussian noise that a diffusion model must resolve. The "fill" is not arbitrary — it is **conditioned** by the text (the prompt) and by the reader's accumulated interpretive resources (the weights).

### 2. Fish's Community = Training Distribution

Stanley Fish argues that readers do not interpret in isolation. Their interpretations are constrained by **interpretive communities** — shared sets of assumptions, conventions, and reading strategies that determine what counts as a legitimate reading.

In the bio-inference model, Fish's interpretive community IS the reader's training distribution. Just as a diffusion model's outputs are bounded by its training data (platform realism), a reader's interpretations are bounded by their community's norms. The reader who has read only romance novels will "denoise" *Moby Dick* differently from the reader who has read only existentialist philosophy.

### 3. Barthes' Readerly/Writerly = Temperature

Roland Barthes distinguishes **readerly** texts (closed, high-certainty, the reader consumes) from **writerly** texts (open, high-indeterminacy, the reader produces). In the bio-inference model:

- **Readerly text** = low-temperature inference: the prompt is so specific that the distribution collapses to a narrow region. One "correct" reading.
- **Writerly text** = high-temperature inference: the prompt is so sparse/ambiguous that the distribution remains wide. Many possible readings. The reader must *produce* the meaning.

Barthes' **jouissance** (bliss/rupture) occurs when the text forces the reader "out of distribution" — demands a generation their existing weights cannot easily support. This is **creative hallucination** in bio-inference: the reader's interpretive framework strains, stretches, and produces something genuinely new.
