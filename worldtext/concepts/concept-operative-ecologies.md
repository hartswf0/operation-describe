## Definition

**Operative ecologies** are conditioning systems that pre-shape what can appear in a field. They are not background context. They are the machinery of appearance itself.

## Three Pre-Operations

| Operation | What It Does |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Pre-select** | Determines what *can* appear at all |
| **Pre-tune** | Sets what *feels* salient in advance |
| **Pre-cut** | Delimits the range of possible action |

Together, these operations **convert environments into fields of readiness**.

## Components

An operative ecology composes at minimum:

- **[[concept-spatial-operator|Spatial Operator]]** — writes encounter geometry
- **Synthetic Sensory Ethnography** — prolongs sensing beyond habit
- **Interface Habit** — the practiced, pre-reflective way users move through interfaces
- **Threshold Logic** — what gates passage, what ranks transitions
- **Attention Routing** — what directs the observer's gaze *before* conscious choice

## Why It Matters

The concept names what traditional ethnography calls "context" but refuses to leave it passive. In hybrid human-machine environments, the field is not a pre-given scene. It is a *produced* arrangement — and operative ecologies are the producing machinery.

This is why [[world-operative-ethnography|operative ethnography]] cannot be participant observation plus technology. The field itself is different in kind.
