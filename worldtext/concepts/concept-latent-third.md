## Definition

**The Latent Third** is the shared aesthetic substrate that forms between the human's **pictorial third** (Louvel's phenomenological image that arises in the reader's mind when text evokes imagery) and the machine's **latent space** (the mathematical manifold where text and image embeddings converge).

Neither fully human nor fully computational, the latent third is the zone where operative ekphrasis actually occurs — where the prompter's mental image and the model's statistical distribution meet, interact, and produce an output that neither could have generated alone.

## Why It Matters

The cosmos had treated the human and the machine as separate agents who interact at the threshold of the prompt interface. The latent third dissolves that boundary: the prompter is already *inside* the latent space (via their mental model of what the prompt will produce), and the latent space is already *inside* the prompter (via the cultural training data the prompter shares with the model).

The latent third is where:
- The prompter's imagination (pictorial third) **guides** the denoising
- The model's statistical distribution **constrains** the imagination
- The output is a **negotiation** between the two, legible to both but owned by neither

## The Blind Ekphrasis Connection

Paper D connects this to Saramago's blind characters describing paintings from memory. The AI user practices **blind ekphrasis**: describing images they have not yet seen, into a darkness (the latent space) they cannot directly inspect. The prompter is permanently blind to the latent space's internal state — they see only outputs, never the manifold itself. All operative ekphrasis is blind navigation.
