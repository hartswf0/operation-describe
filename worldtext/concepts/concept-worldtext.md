## Definition

A **worldtext** is an interactive, spatio-temporal environment specified in language. It is the successor concept to [[concept-imagetext]].

Where Mitchell's imagetext describes a merged verbal-visual object that exceeds the word/image binary, a worldtext expands the operative power of language from configuring pixels in a frame to **acting as a spatial and temporal compiler**. The word no longer produces an image — it produces a *world*: navigable, interactive, temporally unfolding, and governed by rules the text specifies.

## The Threshold: Imagetext → Worldtext

The transition occurs when generative AI moves from images to interactive 3D environments:

| Imagetext | Worldtext |
|-----------|-----------|
| 2D, framed, static | 3D, navigable, temporal |
| Prompt generates a picture | Prompt compiles an environment |
| The viewer sees | The user inhabits |
| Mitchell's ekphrastic hope/fear | Beyond hope/fear: the word IS the world |
| DALL·E, Midjourney | Interactive AI environments, Gaussian splats, Sora-derived worlds |

## The Ur-Text: Homer's Shield

The paper's demonstration example is Homer's Shield of Achilles (Iliad XVIII), which specifies not an image but an entire world in 130 lines: two cities, agriculture, dance, celestial bodies, the river Ocean. The Shield is already a worldtext — a verbal compilation of a navigable cosmos on a physical surface.

The worldtext concept claims this was "always the unstated and unattainable goal" of the ekphrastic tradition: not to describe an image but to **compile a world**.

## The Technological Fork

Two technical paths lead to worldtext:

| Path | Substrate | Nature of "Operative" |
|------|-----------|----------------------|
| **Pure probabilistic** (Sora, Genie) | Video prediction models | The word steers a probability distribution over pixel sequences |
| **Hybrid ontology** (Gaussian splats + deterministic engines) | Structured scene representations | The word specifies objects, physics, rules — it legislates rather than describes |

This fork redefines what "operative" means. In the probabilistic path, the word navigates a latent space. In the hybrid path, the word **governs** a rule system. The hybrid path is closer to the original meaning of "*operative*" — the word that does something — because it specifies not appearance but *behavior*.

## Relationship to the Cosmos

The OPERATION DESCRIBE cosmos is itself a worldtext: a repository of language that compiles an interactive, navigable knowledge world. The Double Barrel visualization is the worldtext's rendered surface. The wiki-link network is its spatial structure. The syntheses are its generated content.

The cosmos is not *about* worldtexts. The cosmos IS one.
