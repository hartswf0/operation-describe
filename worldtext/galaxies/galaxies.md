# Galaxy: Ekphrastic Worlds

> **Species**: galaxy  
> **Scale**: galaxy  
> **Parent**: cosmos  
> **Last Revision**: 2026-04-13  

---

## Definition

The oldest and most gravitationally dense galaxy in the cosmos. Contains all worlds where **language attempts to render visual objects present** — from the ancient rhetorical practice of *enargeia* (making listeners "see" through words alone) through the modern literary ekphrastic tradition to the digital and AI-mediated forms of the 21st century.

## Constituent Systems

- [[system-classical-ekphrasis]] — The paragone tradition from Homer to Ashbery.
- [[system-operative-ekphrasis]] — The collapse of description and generation in AI. *(Shared with galaxy-of-generative-ai-worlds)*

## Constituent World-Types

- [[paragone-representation-world]]
- [[shield-artifact-world]]
- [[operative-ekphrasis-world]] *(shared)*
- [[academic-governance-world]]

## Constituent Worlds

- [[world-classical-ekphrasis]]
- [[world-shield-of-achilles]]
- [[world-operative-ekphrasis]] *(shared)*
- [[world-digital-poetics]]
- [[world-academic-ekphrasis]]

## Governing Symbolic Distinction

**Word / Image** — the gravitational center of this galaxy. All worlds in it orbit the question of what happens when language encounters the visual.

## Deep Variation

The galaxy ranges from Homer's oral performance (800 BCE) to Bajohr's operative ekphrasis in multimodal AI (2024). The word/image distinction is interrogated, upheld, complicated, and collapsed across this span. The galaxy's internal variation is immense — but the structural question never changes: *What does language do when it faces an image?*
