# Atmosphere: moral altitude

> **Species**: atmosphere  
> **Scale**: fragment  
> **Primary Worlds**: [[world-descriptive-struggle]], [[world-breakdown-design]]  
> **Last Revision**: 2026-04-28  

---

The atmosphere of Murdoch's "moral perception" — the air at the altitude where description becomes an ethical act. At this altitude, choosing the right word for another person is not a stylistic decision but a moral one. Calling someone "vulgar" vs. "refreshingly spontaneous" is not merely a change in vocabulary — it is a change in how you orient yourself toward their existence.

This atmosphere thins as altitude increases. At the summit, every description is a moral commitment. This is exhausting. Most people descend. The machine was never at this altitude.
