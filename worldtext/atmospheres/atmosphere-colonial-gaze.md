# Atmosphere: colonial gaze

> **Species**: atmosphere  
> **Scale**: fragment  
> **Primary Worlds**: [[world-thick-description]], [[world-operative-ethnography]]  
> **Last Revision**: 2026-04-28  

---

The atmosphere of power-laden observation. The colonial gaze describes not just the visual field but the asymmetry embedded in who gets to describe whom. Geertz was aware of this: the Western anthropologist describing Balinese culture inherits the structural position of the colonial observer, however well-intentioned.

In the AI context: the training data encodes a colonial gaze when it over-represents certain cultures, aesthetics, bodies, and narratives while under-representing or flattening others. Platform realism is the colonial gaze made statistical — the default world generated by the model is the world as seen from the training distribution's center of mass, which inherits historical patterns of representation.

This atmosphere is not always present, but it haunts any act of cross-cultural description. The question [[conflict-whose-description]] makes it explicit.
