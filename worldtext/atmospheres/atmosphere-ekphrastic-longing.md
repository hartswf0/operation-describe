# Atmosphere: Ekphrastic Longing

> **Species**: atmosphere  
> **Scale**: fragment  
> **Primary World**: [[world-classical-ekphrasis]]  
> **Last Revision**: 2026-04-13  

---

The yearning to make the absent image present through words. Mitchell (1994) theorized this as **ekphrastic hope** — the desire that language might overcome its limits and achieve the immediacy of visual representation. Generated by the structural gap between verbal and visual media: because words can never *be* images, the attempt to make them function *as* images produces a persistent atmosphere of longing and productive failure.

## Generating Structures

- The irreducible gap between word and image ([[distinction-word-image]])
- The paragone tradition that insists on competition between media ([[conflict-paragone]])
- The canonical poems that encode this longing (Keats's "still unravish'd bride of quietness," Ashbery's self-portrait behind convex glass)
