# Memory: Enargeia's Storehouse

> **Species**: memory_trace

## The Trace

Quintilian explains that language with enargeia works because both speaker and listener share a **storehouse of images derived from sense perception.** The orator composes from their own mental store. The listener reconstructs from theirs. The orator's task is to select details that correspond to the listener's prior knowledge — details that activate already-stored images, that conform to expectations, that are "like truth" even if they didn't literally happen.

## Why It Matters

This is the **first model of inference conditioned on prior distribution.** Quintilian's "storehouse" IS the listener's training data. The orator's skill in selecting the "right" details IS prompt engineering — choosing tokens that map to high-confidence regions in the listener's mental embedding space.

The storehouse is also the ancestor of [[concept-viscosity]]. What the listener has never experienced, the orator cannot evoke. The storehouse constrains what enargeia can produce. An orator addressing Roman senators can invoke military formations, judicial procedures, Homeric battles. The same orator cannot invoke images of modern machines or alien landscapes. The storehouse IS the distribution; its limits ARE the viscosity.

When the storehouse is shared widely (a common cultural canon), enargeia works broadly. When it is local (a specialist community), enargeia works narrowly. When it is compressed into statistical weights (a neural network), it works at the scale of the entire internet.
