## What It Is

CLIP (Contrastive Language-Image Pre-training) is the infrastructure that makes operative ekphrasis computationally possible. It is a neural network trained on hundreds of millions of image-caption pairs from the internet, using contrastive learning to map text embeddings and image embeddings into a **single shared vector space.**

In this space, the text "a photo of a cat" and an image of a cat are **nearby vectors.** The word "spiky" is near angular shapes. The word "smooth" is near curved shapes. The space recapitulates the Kiki/Bouba structure because the training data was produced by humans whose language already encodes cross-modal correspondences.

## What It Does For the Cosmos

CLIP IS the ancient dream of enargeia made literal. The progymnasmata student learned which words trigger which visual gestalts in the listener. CLIP learned which tokens trigger which image regions in the latent space. The mechanism is the same: **cross-modal alignment.** The substrate differs (neural tissue vs. silicon). The function is identical: converting verbal signal into visual output.

CLIP is also the infrastructure that makes [[concept-platform-realism]] a structural inevitability. The training data's distribution IS the space's geometry. Stock photography dominates the training data → stock photography dominates the latent space → prompts without strong semantic pressure converge to the training mean. CLIP's embedding space is the **terrain** in which viscosity operates.
