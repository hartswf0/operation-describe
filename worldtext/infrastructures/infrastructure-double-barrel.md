## Definition

The double barrel is the interface architecture that places source and synthesis side by side — the source paper on one screen and the worldtext page on the other, simultaneously visible and mutually legible. Named after the double-barrel shotgun: two outputs from one trigger, both aimed at the same target.

## Why It Matters

The double barrel enforces the Memex Test: can the reader see the trail from synthesis to source? It prevents the common failure mode where syntheses float free of their sources, becoming self-referential abstractions that no longer touch the intellectual reality they claim to describe.

In practice: reading `thick-programming.md` should be done with `geertz.md` open alongside it. Reading `model-measure.md` should be done with `witt.md` beside it. The double barrel makes contact visible and breakage obvious.
