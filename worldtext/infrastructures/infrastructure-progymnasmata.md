## What It Is

The **progymnasmata** were the standardized elementary exercises used to train students in the ancient Greco-Roman rhetorical curriculum. Ekphrasis was one of the more advanced exercises, following narration, confirmation/refutation, praise/blame, comparison, and characterization.

Four canonical authors defined the exercises: **Theon** (1st c. CE), **Hermogenes** (2nd c. CE), **Aphthonios** (4th c. CE), and **Nikolaos** (5th c. CE). Their definitions of ekphrasis are practically identical: *"a speech that leads one around, bringing the subject matter vividly before the eyes."*

## What It Does For the Cosmos

The progymnasmata are the **first system prompt.** They standardize the output format (vivid verbal description), define success criteria (enargeia — does the audience see it?), and provide training data (model descriptions of battles, places, persons, seasons). They are the curriculum that manufactures euphantasiōtoi — people skilled in making others see.

The parallel to modern AI is structural:

| Progymnasmata | AI Training Pipeline |
|---------------|---------------------|
| Standardized exercises | Standardized training data format |
| Model descriptions (battles, seasons) | Training examples (image-caption pairs) |
| Success criterion: enargeia (vividness) | Success criterion: FID score, CLIP similarity |
| Teacher evaluation | Loss function |
| Multiple levels of difficulty | Curriculum learning, progressive training |
| Graduates become orators | Trained models become inference engines |
