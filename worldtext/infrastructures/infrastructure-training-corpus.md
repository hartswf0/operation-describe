## What It Is

The training corpus — LAION-5B, WebImageText, CC12M, and the many other image-text datasets scraped from the internet — is the material substrate of all generative AI imagery. It is the compressed, encoded memory of visual culture, digested and stored as statistical weight distributions across billions of parameters.

## What It Does For the Cosmos

The training corpus IS Fish's interpretive community made literal. It is the accumulated "experience" — the weights — through which the model infers. Its biases are not bugs but structural features: the overrepresentation of stock photography, Western aesthetics, and English-language captions means the model's default output will converge toward these norms. This is [[concept-platform-realism]]'s material basis.

The training corpus is also the source of the model's **cultural memory** — its memory traces. When a prompt invokes "Lotte Reiniger" and the model produces shadow-puppet aesthetics, it is accessing a memory trace of Reiniger's work preserved in the training data. The model remembers what the internet remembers. It forgets what the internet forgets.

The training corpus is the modern equivalent of Quintilian's "store-house of images derived from sense perception." The model's "sense perception" was its exposure to the training data. Its "cultural memory" is whatever was in the data. Its gaps — what was not in the data — are its indeterminacies, its Iserian voids.
