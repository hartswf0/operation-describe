## The Shard

Thetis looks over Hephaestus's shoulder expecting to see vines, olive trees, marble cities. Instead:

> *Barbed wire enclosed an arbitrary spot*  
> *Where bored officials lounged (one cracked a joke)*  
> *And sentries sweated for the day was hot*

The shield does not show the world Thetis wants. It shows the world the maker sees. Hephaestus is the first prompter who produces an image that disappoints its audience — not through failure, but through fidelity to a different reality.

## Why It Matters

Auden's poem is the most powerful existing artifact of **reclassification in ekphrasis** — Geertz's Layer 6 made literal. Thetis has an audience expectation (Layer 4): ritual pieties, games, dancers. What the maker generates demolishes that expectation. The shield's content is reclassified at the threshold of Thetis's gaze — from divine craft to evidence of atrocity.

This is exactly what happens when a generated AI image crosses from the prompter's context to a hostile reviewer's context. The image does not change. The frame changes. The meaning changes. The barbed wire was always on the shield. Thetis just expected olive trees.

**The ragged urchin** — "aimless and alone," who has never heard "of any world where promises were kept" — is the AI model: a pattern-completer with no moral framework, for whom violence is axiomatic because it is statistically dominant in the training data.
