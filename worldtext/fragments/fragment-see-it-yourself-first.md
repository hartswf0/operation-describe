# Fragment: "See It Yourself First"

> **Species**: fragment  
> **Scale**: fragment

> **Source**: Quintilian, *Institutio Oratoria* 6.2.29-32

## The Shard

Quintilian instructs the orator: to produce enargeia, you must first **see the scene yourself** in your mind's eye. Only then will your language activate the same mental imagery in your listener. The orator who has not visualized cannot make others visualize. The speech must be composed from the inside of the image, not from the outside.

## Why It Matters

This is the first recorded description of **prompt composition as internal inference**. The orator runs the image through their own bio-inference engine before encoding it into language. They are, in Batesonian terms, performing Learning I and Learning II simultaneously: generating the image (I) AND modeling the listener's likely generation (II).

In modern terms: **the thick prompter must run the prompt through their own mental model before submitting it to the machine.** The prompter who cannot picture the intended output cannot compose a prompt that will navigate to it. Quintilian's instruction is the ancient form of "know what you want before you prompt."
