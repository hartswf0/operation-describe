## The Shard

`tēn akoēn opsin poiōn` — **"turning hearing into sight."**

This is the oldest surviving definition of ekphrasis's purpose. Not "describing an image." Not "representing a visual artwork." **Converting one sensory modality into another.** The ears become eyes. The acoustic signal becomes a visual experience.

## Why It Matters

This fragment is the cosmos's origin point. Everything in the WorldText — from Quintilian's enargeia to CLIP's cross-modal alignment to the prompter typing into a text box — is a technical elaboration of this six-word phrase. The word does not describe sight. The word **produces** sight. In the listener's mind (antiquity), in the reader's imagination (modernity), in the latent space (now).

The fragment contains the entire thesis of operative ekphrasis in compressed form. Bajohr didn't invent operativity — pseudo-Longinos named it two thousand years ago, in six words.
