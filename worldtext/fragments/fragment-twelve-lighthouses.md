## The Shard

When autonomous text-image-text loops run without human intervention — reactive ekphrasis with no steersman — the output converges to approximately **12 generic motifs**: lighthouses, coastal sunsets, lone trees, misty mountains, silhouetted figures at golden hour. Hintze et al. call this **"visual elevator music."**

## Why It Matters

The twelve lighthouses are the empirical proof of [[concept-viscosity]]. Left to their own gradient descent, the loops slide down the loss surface toward the lowest-energy configurations — the most-represented visual concepts in the training data. The twelve lighthouses ARE the local minima of the latent space's topology. They are the steady states of the prompt-image-feedback system.

The human contribution to operative ekphrasis is therefore not optional. Without the steersman's evaluative intervention, the loop degenerates. The "creative act" in generative AI is **resistance to convergence** — the force the prompter exerts against the gradient. Remove the human from the loop and you get not art but the aestheticized average.

**The twelve lighthouses are the Auden shield's barbed wire, viewed from the computational side.** The model's default is not beauty or originality but **statistical mediocrity**. The thick prompter is the one who prevents the twelve lighthouses from appearing.
