## The Shard

> *If traditional ekphrasis was a sharp blade trying to cut through the silence of the image, AI is a "blunt sword that cuts through the Gordian knot of the natural sign." It solves the problem by dissolving it.*

## Why It Matters

The metaphor captures the paradox of vindication/vitiation. Operative ekphrasis fulfills Mitchell's "ekphrastic hope" — the word CAN produce the image. But in succeeding, it **spoils the game.** The aesthetic pleasure of classical ekphrasis depended on the impossibility of the task: the poet struggling, beautifully, to make words do what paint does. When the machine succeeds instantly, that productive failure — that generative gap between word and image — vanishes.

The blunt sword is the image of a **pyrrhic victory**. The paragone is won. The cost is the paragone itself.
