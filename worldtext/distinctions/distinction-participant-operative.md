# Participant / Operative

> **Species**: distinction  
> **Poles**: participant observer / operative observer  
> **Parent world**: [[world-operative-ethnography]]

---

## The Distinction

| | Participant Observer | Operative Observer |
|---|---------------------|-------------------|
| **Posture** | Joins field | Edits field formation |
| **Assumption** | Field precedes observation | Field is co-produced by observation |
| **Description** | Follows encounter (trace logic) | Re-enters encounter (feedback logic) |
| **Agency** | Located in human subject | Distributed across body, tool, protocol, interface, model |
| **Unit** | Co-presence | Configured encounter |
| **Output** | Account of what happened | Worldtext that recirculates as new condition |

## Why It Matters

Participant observation is the foundational method of ethnography. It assumes a basic order: first there is a field, then there is an encounter, then there is a description. Observation follows the world, and writing follows observation.

In hybrid environments shaped by interfaces, prompts, models, and feedback loops, that order no longer holds. The field is partly configured by the means through which it becomes perceptible. The operative observer recognizes this and **works on the configuration itself** rather than pretending to merely witness it.
