## Poles

| Pole | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Residue** | The inert artifacts that remain when theory is lost — wikis, lore bibles, prompt libraries, documentation. They can be read but not operated. |
| **Theory** | The living operational possession that lets an operator explain, justify, modify, teach, and deliver without introducing contradiction. |

## The Distinction

```
<residue> [is-read]
<theory> [is-operated]

<program_text> [is] <residue>
<program_theory> [is] <living_possession>

<wiki> [is] <residue>
<worldtext> [is] <theory>
```

A wiki stores facts. A Worldtext stores commitments. Facts can be retrieved without understanding their consequences. Commitments cannot be modified without understanding their propagation.

## The Naur Test

If you can answer all five:
1. Why is this rule shaped this way?
2. What will break if it changes?
3. How would you teach this to a new operator?
4. Where does this rule interact with other rules?
5. What evidence justified this rule's existence?

...then you possess the theory. If you can only answer #1, you possess residue.
