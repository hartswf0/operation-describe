# Distinction: collage / assemblage

> **Species**: symbolic_distinction  
> **Scale**: fragment  
> **Primary World**: [[world-intermediality]]  
> **Last Revision**: 2026-04-28  

---

**Poles**: collage / assemblage

Collage arranges fragments on a surface — they remain visible as fragments. The seams show. The viewer sees both the parts and the combination. Assemblage integrates fragments into a new whole — the parts lose their individual identity. The seams disappear. The viewer sees only the result.

AI image generation is assemblage, not collage. The training images are dissolved into weights. No individual source image survives in recognizable form. The output does not show seams. This makes provenance invisible — a political problem, not just an aesthetic one.
