**Poles**: guardrail / hydrology

A guardrail is an external constraint bolted onto a system to prevent bad outcomes. It says: do not go here. A hydrology is the internal flow-structure of the system itself — the channels, pressures, gradients, and attractors that determine where output naturally goes. Guardrails are visible, brittle, and circumventable. Hydrology is invisible, robust, and constitutive.

Most AI safety discourse operates at the guardrail level: content policies, RLHF, refusal training. The hydrology — the training distribution's internal pressure toward certain aesthetics, certain narratives, certain kinds of people — is the deeper governance. You can add a guardrail that says "do not produce harmful content." But the hydrology of the latent space already channels output toward stock photography, golden hour, and nostalgic vibes. The guardrail prevents specific harms. The hydrology determines the default world.
