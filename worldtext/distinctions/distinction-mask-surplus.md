**Poles**: mask / surplus

A mask is the structure that constrains and shapes. A surplus is the remainder that exceeds the structure. Every generative system produces both: the mask (the genre, the template, the learned distribution, the prompt constraint) and the surplus (the affect, the uncanny, the ghost, the thing that was not asked for but arrived).

Calvino's "machinery plus ghosts" IS the mask/surplus distinction: the machinery is the mask, the ghost is the surplus. Propp's morphology is the mask of folktale; the particular chill of a well-told version is the surplus. The latent space is the mask of trained correlations; the generated image that stops the operator cold is the surplus.

The critical question for AI aesthetics: is the surplus genuine (produced by the intersection of constraints in ways the operator couldn't predict) or illusory (a pattern the operator simply didn't recognize as familiar)?
