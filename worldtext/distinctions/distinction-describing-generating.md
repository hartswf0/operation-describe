**Poles**: describing / generating

To describe is to undergo the pressure of a reality that resists easy formulation — to struggle toward adequacy. To generate is to produce probable continuations from learned patterns — to output without undergoing. The distinction is not between human and machine but between costly and costless language. A human can generate (filling a template, recycling phrases). A machine could in principle describe (if it bore the cost of inadequacy). The poles name a difference in what the language event costs the producer.

This is the foundational distinction of the entire cosmos. Every other distinction in the worldtext is a local application of this one:
