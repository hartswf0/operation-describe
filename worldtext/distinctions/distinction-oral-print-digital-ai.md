**Poles**: oral / print / digital / AI (four-pole spectrum, not a binary)

Each media regime produces a different relationship between the producer, the audience, the archive, and the authority of the text:

| Regime | Producer | Archive | Authority | Viscosity |
|--------|----------|---------|-----------|-----------|
| **Oral** | Performer-in-presence | Memory + formula | Communal validation | High (must be embodied) |
| **Print** | Author-in-absence | Library + citation | Institutional peer review | Medium-high (must be composed) |
| **Digital** | User-in-network | Database + search | Platform metrics | Medium-low (copy-paste, remix) |
| **AI** | Operator-in-circuit | Training data + weights | None defined yet | Very low (prompt → output) |

The transition from each regime to the next reduces viscosity and increases speed while losing a specific form of accountability:
- Oral → Print: gains fixity, loses embodied presence
- Print → Digital: gains speed, loses institutional gatekeeping
- Digital → AI: gains generation, loses authorial accountability

The worldtext project sits at the AI pole but tries to import the accountability structures of Print and the embodied struggle of Oral. This is its central design tension.
