## Resolution

The distinction is **scale-dependent**, not absolute:

- At the **technical level**, the prompt produces — it conditions the denoising process, generating a unique pixel configuration that did not previously exist
- At the **cultural level**, the product is constrained — the prompt navigates toward high-probability regions of a distribution shaped by training data demographics, platform economics, and absorbed aesthetic conventions
- At the **aesthetic level**, the distinction maps onto [[distinction-seamless-exposed]]: the seamless path navigates (toward photographic plausibility), the exposed path produces (by foregrounding the generation process itself)

If images are "found through words" rather than produced by them (Meyer), is operative ekphrasis a misnomer? The cosmos's answer: the "operative" act is real at the computational level (the word conditions the generation) but constrained at the cultural level (the space of possible generations was pre-structured). **Both descriptions are true at different scales.**
