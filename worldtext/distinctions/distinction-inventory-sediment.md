**Poles**: inventory / sediment

An inventory is a list of what is present: countable, arrangeable, exhaustible. A sediment is what has accumulated through time: layered, compressed, partially illegible, never fully recoverable. Calvino's storyteller works from an inventory (finite elements, combinable). But the latent space of a trained model is sediment (billions of cultural artifacts compressed into weights, their individual identities dissolved into statistical proximity).

The distinction matters because inventory-thinking treats the training data as a list of items. Sediment-thinking treats it as a geological formation: the individual items are gone, but their combined pressure shaped the topology of the space.
