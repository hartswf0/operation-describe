**Poles**: autographic / allographic

Nelson Goodman's distinction. An autographic work (painting, handmade print) is authenticated by its history of production — a forgery is a different work even if visually identical. An allographic work (musical score, text) is authenticated by compliance with a notation system — any correct performance is equally authentic.

AI-generated images collapse this distinction. They have no autographic history (no hand touched them). They have no allographic notation (no score specifies them). They exist in a third category: **statistically probable surfaces generated from compressed training data**. A "forgery" of an AI image is meaningless because there is no original to forge. An exact duplicate is not a copy because there was no singular production event.
