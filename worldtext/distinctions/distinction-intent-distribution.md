## The New Paragone

The ancient paragone was: **word vs. image** — which medium is superior?

The modern paragone is: **intent vs. distribution** — does the prompter's will or the training data's gravity determine the output?

This is the structural successor to the paragone. It does not pit medium against medium but **agency against inertia** — the creative will of the individual against the statistical weight of the collective.

| Old Paragone | New Paragone |
|-------------|-------------|
| Word vs. Image | Intent vs. Distribution |
| Which medium wins? | Which force wins? |
| Resolved by operative ekphrasis (collapse) | Unresolvable — constitutive tension |
| The agon between rival representational systems | The agon between creativity and convergence |
| Da Vinci vs. poets | The prompter vs. the latent space |

## Why It Is Unresolvable

The old paragone could be "resolved" by Bajohr's operative ekphrasis: word and image become the same data type, so the competition ends.

The new paragone cannot be resolved because intent and distribution are not rival systems but **co-constitutive forces**:
- Without intent, you get the 12 lighthouses (Hintze et al.)
- Without distribution, you get noise (the prompt has nothing to navigate)
- The output is ALWAYS a negotiation between the two
- What varies is the **ratio**: how much intent vs. how much distribution

**The thick prompter is the person who maximizes intent within the distribution.** They do not escape the distribution — that is impossible. They steer within it, the way Cohen steered within the mezrag system, the way the orator steered within the cultural memory of the audience. Creative agency is real. It is also bounded. This is Geertz's lesson: meaning is made, but it is made within a web of significance that the maker did not weave.

## The Viscosity Interpretation

In [[concept-viscosity]] terms:
- **Intent** = the force applied by the prompt (pushes the generation in a specific direction)
- **Distribution** = the viscosity of the medium (resists the push, pulls toward the center)
- **Thick prompting** = increasing the force (more semantic pressure, more specificity)
- **Context engineering** = reducing the viscosity (restructuring the environment to ease the path)
- **Convergence** = the limit when force → 0 (autonomous loops, no human)
- **Hallucination** = the limit when viscosity → 0 (no constraints, the model free-associates)

The creative zone is the mid-range: enough intent to steer, enough distribution to provide material.

## Scale-Dependence

Like [[distinction-navigation-production]], this distinction is **scale-dependent**:

- **At the token level**: intent dominates — each token meaningfully conditions the next step
- **At the session level**: negotiation — intent and distribution interact across iterations
- **At the population level**: distribution dominates — billions of users collectively reproduce the mean
- **At the historical level**: intent accumulates — thick prompters shift the distribution over time (the way great writers shift interpretive communities over generations)
