# Distinction: good intermediality / bad intermediality

> **Species**: symbolic_distinction  
> **Scale**: fragment  
> **Primary World**: [[world-intermediality]]  
> **Last Revision**: 2026-04-28  

---

**Poles**: good intermediality / bad intermediality

Good intermediality: the collision between media produces meaning that neither medium alone could carry. Word and image together create a third thing. The dissonance between them is productive. Mitchell's "imagetext" is good intermediality — the slash between image/text marks a generative tension.

Bad intermediality: the collision between media produces only confusion, noise, or the illusion of depth. Two media are placed side by side but do not interact. The juxtaposition is cosmetic, not structural. Much "multimedia" presentation is bad intermediality — slides with stock photos alongside bullet points, where the image could be removed without changing the argument.

The test: remove one medium. If the remaining medium says the same thing, the intermediality was decorative. If something has been lost that cannot be recovered, the intermediality was genuine.
