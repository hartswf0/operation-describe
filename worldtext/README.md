# OPERATION DESCRIBE — WorldText Cosmos

> *To describe was once to stand outside and look in.  
> Now to describe is to generate, and to generate is to describe.*

**OPERATION DESCRIBE** is a knowledge cosmos built from the collision of three forces: **ekphrasis** (the 2,500-year-old practice of making the absent visible through language), **thick description** (Geertz's method of rendering cultural meaning as densely observable texture), and **generative AI** (machines that collapse the distinction between describing and producing images, worlds, and texts).

The governing thesis: operative ekphrasis is not new — it is the ancient capacity of language restored at computational scale. Homer's *Shield of Achilles* was always a worldtext. We just didn't have the substrate to run it.

## What This Is

This repository is the evidence base and worldbuilding substrate for the paper **"From Imagetext to Worldtext: Generative AI as Operative Ekphrasis"** (Hartsoe & Bolter, 2026). It is simultaneously:

- A **research archive** — 708 sources spanning 1903–2026, from Homer scholarship through transformer architecture
- A **knowledge cosmos** — 374 structured pages organized into 26 species (worlds, entities, bridges, syntheses, concepts, thresholds, etc.)
- A **worldtext** — the cosmos is not *about* worldtexts; it *is* one. The wiki-link network is its spatial structure. The Double-Bar visualization is its rendered surface.

The corpus covers: ekphrasis theory, thick description methodology, generative AI and diffusion models, prompt engineering, world models, intermediality studies, digital humanities, and computational creativity.

## The Central Claim

> **Operative ekphrasis is not an invention. It is a recovery.**  
> The ancient definition of ekphrasis was already operative — words produced visual experience, not just described pre-existing images. The 1955 Spitzer narrowing concealed this by redefining ekphrasis as "verbal representation of visual representation." Generative AI restores the ancient operation computationally: text becomes image, not as metaphor but as executable instruction.
>
> **The imagetext has arrived. But its arrival reveals the new paragone: intent vs. distribution.** The prompter's creative will against the training data's statistical gravity. Remove the human from the loop and you get twelve lighthouses — the platform mean at rest. Restore the human and you get the carrot uprising.
>
> **The worldtext is what operative ekphrasis becomes when it stops generating images and starts compiling worlds.** Homer already knew. The Shield of Achilles was always a worldtext. We just didn't have the substrate to run it.
