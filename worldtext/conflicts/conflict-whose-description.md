## The Conflict

Every description is authored. Every author has a position. The question "whose description?" asks who gets to define the terms, set the frame, choose the vocabulary, and declare what counts as adequate.

In traditional ethnography: the Western anthropologist describes the non-Western culture. Geertz knew this was a power relation. His thick description tried to read the culture on its own terms — but the reader is still Geertz, the vocabulary is still English, the publication is still Princeton.

In AI: the model describes the world through its training data. Whose descriptions trained it? Whose aesthetics dominate the latent space? Whose languages are underrepresented? Whose cultural forms are flattened into "style"? The model is a description machine that inherits every bias of its training archive without knowing it has biases.

In this cosmos: the syntheses describe the papers. But the describer (the operator + AI system) may not have the same relationship to the source material that the original authors did. The question is whether the worldtext's descriptions are accountable to their sources or are floating summaries that have detached from the intellectual worlds they claim to represent.
