## The Conflict

Wittgenstein says the model must be "laid against reality like a measure" — it must make **contact**. Ricoeur says the text survives its author and enters the world of unknown readers — it achieves meaning through **distance**. Contact produces accuracy. Distance produces interpretation. Both are required. Neither is sufficient.

The worldtext needs contact (the syntheses must touch the source papers, the entities must correspond to real thinkers, the worlds must map actual intellectual landscapes). The worldtext also needs distance (the syntheses must say more than the sources, the atlas must generate new patterns, the programs must produce insights their operators did not foresee).

The conflict is: when contact and distance pull in opposite directions — when being faithful to the source (contact) prevents the synthesis from becoming its own thing (distance) — which wins?
