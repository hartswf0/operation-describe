## The Conflict

C.P. Snow's "two cultures" (1959): the sciences and the humanities do not speak to each other. In this cosmos the conflict takes a specific form: the PAPERS are humanities scholarship (Geertz, Ryle, Murdoch, Ricoeur, Calvino). The subject MATTER is technical (language models, latent spaces, diffusion pipelines, embedding vectors). The worldtext tries to bridge them — to bring humanistic critique to bear on technical systems.

The danger on the humanities side: using technical terms decoratively ("latent space" as metaphor, "vector" as poetry) without understanding the mathematics.

The danger on the technical side: treating humanistic concepts as mere commentary ("thick description" as a buzzword, "accountability" as a compliance checkbox) without undergoing the intellectual traditions that produced them.

The worldtext's position: the bridge is not built by diluting either side. It is built by being rigorous in BOTH — by insisting that the humanistic concepts make real contact with the technical systems, and that the technical systems are subjected to genuine humanistic interrogation.
