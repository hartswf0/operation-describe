## The Conflict

Every system of organization — worldtext included — domesticates the wild material it ingests. The source papers are wild: they wander, digress, contradict, circle back, surprise. The atlas is domestic: it taxonomizes, links, categorizes, resolves. The conflict is between the generative wildness of the original thinking and the organizational domestication that makes it navigable.

Calvino saw this in narrative: the tribal storyteller's performance was wild (improvised, responsive, alive). The Proppian morphology is domesticated (fixed functions, sequenced slots). The morphology enables analysis but kills the performance. Lévi-Strauss's transformation system is an attempt to domesticate myth without killing it — but the tension remains.

In AI: the latent space is wild (immense, non-linear, full of unpredictable adjacencies). The interface domesticates it (prompt box, output frame, safety filter). The thick prompter works at the boundary — using domestic tools (language, parameters) to access wild territory (latent adjacencies the training never explicitly represented).

This conflict cannot be resolved. It can only be managed. The worldtext must be domestic enough to be navigable and wild enough to produce surprise.
