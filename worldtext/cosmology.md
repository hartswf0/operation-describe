## Universe Statement

OPERATION DESCRIBE is a cosmos built from the collision of two ancient practices and one emergent force: **ekphrasis** (making the absent visible through language), **thick description** (rendering cultural meaning as densely observable texture), and **generative AI** (machines that collapse the distinction between describing and producing images, worlds, and texts). The 708-source evidence stratum spans 1903–2026 and constitutes one of the most comprehensive research archives on the interzone where verbal representation meets visual production in the age of neural networks.

The governing tension: **to describe was once to stand outside and look in; now to describe is to generate, and to generate is to describe.** The old paragone — word versus image — does not vanish. It implodes, forming new topologies of creative authority, semiotic collapse, and world-making power.

## Dominant Galaxies

### 1. [[galaxy-of-ekphrastic-worlds]]
The oldest gravitational center. Includes all worlds where language attempts to render visual objects present — from Homer's Shield of Achilles through Keats and Auden to contemporary digital poetics. Contains the classical paragone tension (word vs. image) as its nuclear fuel.

### 2. [[galaxy-of-thick-description-worlds]]
Rooted in Geertz's ethnographic revolution: the commitment to describing cultural phenomena with enough semiotic density that the *meaning* — not just the behavior — becomes legible. Extends into philosophical hermeneutics, phenomenology of experience, design research, and AI evaluation frameworks.

### 3. [[galaxy-of-generative-ai-worlds]]
The newest and most volatile galaxy. Contains worlds of text-to-image generation, latent space navigation, prompt engineering/craft, world models, diffusion architectures, multimodal neural networks, and the computational infrastructure that makes operative ekphrasis possible.

### 4. [[galaxy-of-intermedial-worlds]]
The interstitial galaxy connecting all others. Worlds of media theory, intersemiotic translation, remediation, multimodality, and the persistent question of what happens at the borders between sign systems.

## Active World-Types

1. **[[paragone-representation-world]]** — Worlds where word and image compete for representational authority.
2. **[[latent-space-navigation-world]]** — Worlds where agents navigate continuous vector spaces to materialize visual or textual outputs.
3. **[[thick-description-world]]** — Worlds where the primary labor is rendering meaning legible through dense semiotic layering.
4. **[[prompt-craft-world]]** — Worlds where the human-machine interface is the prompt, and craft replaces engineering.
5. **[[operative-ekphrasis-world]]** — Worlds where describing and generating collapse into a single act.
6. **[[intermedial-threshold-world]]** — Worlds organized around the crossing between media, sign systems, or representational modes.
7. **[[shield-artifact-world]]** — Worlds organized around a central ekphrastic object that anchors an entire cosmology (Shield of Achilles, Keats's urn, Hopper's Nighthawks).
8. **[[hallucinated-environment-world]]** — Worlds where AI agents train inside their own generated realities.
9. **[[academic-governance-world]]** — Worlds governed by peer review, citation, canon formation, and disciplinary legitimacy.
10. **[[semiotic-collapse-world]]** — Worlds where previously distinct sign systems (text/image, description/production) implode into each other.

## Unresolved Frontiers

1. **The Ekphrastic Singularity** — At what point does operative ekphrasis (Bajohr 2024) fully dissolve the classical distinction between describing and making? Is there a residue? What survives?
2. **Thick Description of AI Outputs** — Can Geertz's method be applied to generated images? What would it mean to "thickly describe" a diffusion model's output? Who is the culture being described? (Partially answered: [[thick-description-of-diffusion]]).
3. **The Paragone After Collapse** — If multimodal AI encodes text and image as the same type of information, what new forms of tension replace the old paragone? Answer emerging: [[conflict-abundance-vs-platform-realism]].
4. **Prompt as Ritual** — Is prompting a new form of ekphrastic ritual? What stabilizes it? What governance constrains it?
5. **World Models as Ekphrasis** — Ha & Schmidhuber's "World Models" (2018) train agents in hallucinated dreams. Is the model's internal world an ekphrastic object? Or is it the viewer?
6. **The Missing Atmospheric Layer** — Most computational and design research in the corpus lacks atmospheric density. How does one thicken the description of a latent space?
7. **The Platform Realism Challenge** — If prompts navigate rather than produce (Meyer), what exactly is the "operative" act? Does the word create or find? The tension between [[concept-abundance]] and [[concept-platform-realism]] is the central unresolved conflict.
8. **The Photorealism Problem** — Three new sources (Steyerl, Wasielewski, Meyer) show the seamless path is epistemologically impossible: AI cannot produce indexical images because the [[concept-synthetic-camera]] has no lens. What the seamless path produces is the *appearance* of the real, not the real.
9. **The Convergence Problem** — If autonomous reactive loops converge to "visual elevator music" (Hintze et al.), the human contribution to operative ekphrasis is not optional — it is constitutive. What exactly is that contribution?
10. **From Imagetext to Worldtext** — At what point does an interactive 3D environment cease to be "ekphrastic"? The [[concept-worldtext]] extends the operative power of language from pixels to worlds, but the theoretical framework has not yet caught up.
