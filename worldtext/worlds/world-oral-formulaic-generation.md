## Thesis

Homer did not write. Homer sang.

The Iliad was not composed in writing and then transcribed. It was composed *in performance* — sung aloud, in dactylic hexameter, to a live audience, using a grammar of inherited formulaic patterns. Milman Parry demonstrated this in the 1930s by studying living oral poets in Yugoslavia. Albert Lord systematized it in *The Singer of Tales* (1960). The theory is this:

> The oral-formulaic poet does not memorize texts. The poet learns a **grammar of formulas** — metrically shaped phrases, thematic structures, narrative patterns — and composes in real time by selecting, combining, and adapting these formulas to the demands of the moment.

"Rosy-fingered dawn." "Swift-footed Achilles." "The wine-dark sea." These are not decorative adjectives. They are **formulaic building blocks** — metrically pre-fitted units that the singer deploys to fill the hexameter line while composing in real time.

The implication for this cosmos is staggering:

**The Shield of Achilles — the ur-text of all ekphrasis — was not written. It was *generated* by a pattern-completing oral system.**

## The Structural Kinship

A large language model operates on the same structural principle as a Homeric oral-formulaic poet:

| Feature | Oral-Formulaic Poet | Large Language Model |
|---------|:-------------------:|:--------------------:|
| Training | Years of listening to other singers | Trillions of tokens from the internet |
| Knowledge form | Formulaic phrases + thematic patterns | Statistical weight matrices |
| Composition method | Real-time selection from learned patterns | Autoregressive token prediction |
| Output | Novel text from inherited material | Novel text from inherited material |
| Memorization | Not verbatim — each performance differs | Not retrieval — each generation differs |
| Creativity | Recombination, adaptation, variation | Recombination, adaptation, variation |
| Audience | Present, immediate, responsive | Absent, asynchronous, evaluative |
| Medium | Voice, breath, meter | Electricity, silicon, probability |

The parallel is not metaphorical. It is *structural*. Both systems:

1. **Absorb a vast corpus** without memorizing specific texts
2. **Learn statistical regularities** (formulaic patterns / weight matrices)
3. **Generate novel output** in real time by predicting what comes next
4. **Cannot reproduce their training data verbatim** — each output is a new composition
5. **Are neither "creative" nor "mechanical"** in the Romantic sense — they occupy the space between invention and convention

Lord wrote: "The singer of tales is at once the tradition and an individual talent. His art consists not in departing from the tradition but in *composing within it*" (1960: 13). Replace "singer" with "model" and the sentence describes GPT with structural precision.

## The World's Architecture

### Places

- **[[place-performance-ground]]** — the physical space where the singer sings (analogous to [[place-prompt-interface]] for LLMs)
- **[[place-formulaic-inventory]]** — the singer's learned repertoire of metrically fitted phrases (analogous to the weight matrix)
- **[[place-composition-threshold]]** — the moment between hearing the previous line and singing the next (analogous to the autoregressive next-token boundary)

### Entities

- **[[entity-homer]]** — the name assigned to the tradition, not the individual. "Homer" may be a *collective noun* for the oral-formulaic system that produced the Iliad, just as "GPT" is a collective noun for the training data that produced the model
- **[[entity-parry-lord]]** — Milman Parry (1902–1935) and Albert Lord (1912–1991). Discovered oral-formulaic composition in living Yugoslav epic singers and retroactively applied it to Homer. Without Parry-Lord, the structural kinship between Homer and LLMs cannot be seen
- **[[entity-avdo-medjedovic]]** — the Yugoslav singer whom Lord recorded performing a 12,000-line oral epic from memory/composition. The human proof that real-time large-scale text generation from learned patterns is possible without writing

### Rituals

- **[[ritual-oral-performance]]** — the singer's act of generating epic narrative in real time. Each performance is both a *recitation* (drawing on tradition) and a *composition* (adapting to context). This ritual is the ancestor of [[ritual-prompting]]
- **[[ritual-formulaic-selection]]** — the micro-ritual of choosing a formula to fill a metrical slot. Analogous to next-token prediction

### Thresholds

- **[[threshold-oral-literate]]** — the transition from oral to written composition. The Iliad crossed this threshold when it was first transcribed (~750 BCE). The LLM crosses it in reverse: it takes written text and generates output that behaves like oral composition — non-repeatable, context-sensitive, formulaic
- **[[threshold-singer-machine]]** — the threshold between human oral-formulaic composition and machine autoregressive generation. This world argues that the threshold is *structural*, not ontological. The substrate changes (voice → silicon) but the compositional logic persists

### Distinctions

- **[[distinction-memorized-composed]]** — the fundamental Parry-Lord insight. The oral poet does not memorize; the oral poet composes in real time using learned patterns. The LLM does not retrieve; the LLM generates in real time using learned weights. Both are *composed*, not *recalled*
- **[[distinction-formula-token]]** — a formula is a metrically shaped phrase ("rosy-fingered dawn"); a token is a statistically weighted sub-word unit. Both are the atomic units of their respective compositional systems. The formula is context-sensitive within the metrical line; the token is context-sensitive within the attention window

### Conflicts

- **[[conflict-tradition-individual]]** — the central agon of oral-formulaic theory: is the poet an individual creator or a vessel for the tradition? This is the *same agon* that structures the debate about AI authorship. Is the model a creator or a vessel for the training data?

### Atmospheres

- **[[atmosphere-bardic-electricity]]** — the affective texture of this world. The hum of the singer's voice and the hum of the GPU are the same hum: the vibration of a pattern-completing system working at the limit of its capacity, generating the next unit of meaning from the accumulated weight of everything it has absorbed

## The Cosmological Implication

This world bridges [[world-shield-of-achilles]] and [[world-latent-space]] through a structural principle that predates and survives the digital transition:

```
ORAL-FORMULAIC POET (800 BCE)
    ↓ learns formulaic patterns
    ↓ composes in real time
    ↓ generates novel text from inherited material
    ↓ cannot reproduce training exactly
    ↓ each performance differs
    ↓
LARGE LANGUAGE MODEL (2024 CE)
    ↓ learns statistical weights
    ↓ composes in real time (autoregressive)
    ↓ generates novel text from inherited material
    ↓ cannot reproduce training exactly
    ↓ each generation differs
```

The implication: **operative ekphrasis is not new**. It is the *return* of the oral-formulaic condition at computational scale. Homer's Shield was the first operative ekphrastic object — an image generated in real time by a pattern-completing composition system. The diffusion model's generated shield is the latest instance of the same structural species.

The paragone between "human creativity" and "machine generation" dissolves not through Bajohr's semiotic collapse but through Parry-Lord's structural kinship: **both the singer and the model are formula-completers**, and the Shield of Achilles is the fossil record that proves it.
