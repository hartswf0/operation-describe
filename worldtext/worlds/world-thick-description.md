## Ontology

A world of density. Clifford Geertz (1973) drew the distinction between a **thin description** (recording that someone contracted an eyelid) and a **thick description** (interpreting that contraction as a wink — a deliberate, culturally meaningful gesture within a shared symbolic code). Thick description renders the **semiotic layers** of cultural action legible. It does not explain behavior from the outside; it reads meaning from the inside.

This world extends far beyond ethnography. Thick description has become a methodological aspiration in hermeneutics, design research, AI evaluation, literary criticism, and any domain where the difference between *what happened* and *what it meant* is the central problem.

## Resource Flows

- **Fieldwork time** — The scarcest resource. Thickness requires immersion.
- **Interpretive authority** — Flows from the ethnographer's depth of engagement.
- **Reading lists** — Geertz's "thick description" became one of the most taught texts in social science.

## Conflict Grammars

- **Description vs. explanation** — Geertz insisted thick description is *interpretation*, not causal explanation. But the boundary is perennially contested.
- **Thickness vs. verifiability** — How thick is thick enough? When does thick description become overinterpretation?
- **Travelling concepts** — Bal (2002) argues thick description has travelled far from anthropology. Is it still the same method when applied to AI evaluation or design research?
- **Thickness invoked but not delivered** — [[world-thin-research]] argues that HCI invokes Geertz but produces only thin descriptions: interview transcripts, coded themes, affinity diagrams. The method that demands immersion is practiced through extraction. Polanyi's tacit knowledge ("we can know more than we can tell") is the executioner of the interview-based approach.
- **Deep play in the latent space** — [[world-latent-wrestling]] extends Geertz's cockfight into the generative epoch. The prompt session is deep play: aesthetic capital wagered against the statistical mean of the internet. The Arena is the new cockfight ring.

## Memory Traces

- Ryle's original wink/twitch distinction (1968) persists as the foundational anecdote.
- Geertz's Balinese cockfight (1973) is the most-cited ethnographic scene in social science.
- The "thick evaluation" discourse (2020s) — applying Geertzian density to AI fairness and cultural representation — is a living memory trace.

## Time Structure

**Diffusive / Persistent**: Thick description was articulated in 1973 and has never stopped spreading. It has crossed from anthropology to literary criticism, philosophy, design, HCI, and AI evaluation. Each crossing deposits a new sediment layer without erasing the older ones.
