## Ontology

A world where the path IS the scholarship. Vannevar Bush's 1945 "As We May Think" diagnoses a crisis not of ignorance but of routing: important work disappears inside the mass of the merely available. Mendel's genetics existed but never reached the right readers. The problem is not that information is absent — it is that the right relation to the record does not exist.

Bush's solution is not bigger storage but a durable trail: a path of association built by a reader through a field of documents, preservable, transmissible, and inspectable. The scholar's work is not only to read but to connect. The trail is both method and memory.

The central question of this world: **can a knowledge system show not only what it says, but how its path through the record was built?**

## Places

- **The memex desk** — Bush's imagined device: a personal library with linked, annotatable, trail-bearing document pairs
- **The trail** — a durable associative path binding two items, nameable and replayable
- **The Turkish bow** — Bush's example: a research trail from bow properties through elasticity, physical constants, personal analysis, and conversation, transmissible to a friend
- **The generative opaque** — the contemporary anti-memex: an AI system that produces answers but hides the associative path

## Key Entities

- **Vannevar Bush** — "As We May Think" (1945): the trail as the unit of scholarly work
- The **repetitive/mature thought distinction** — machines handle repetitive operations (calculation, sorting, indexing); humans handle mature judgment (selection, interpretation, analogy). AI blurs this line.

## Conflict Grammars

- **Storage vs. consultation** — having the record is not the same as using it. A million documents compressed into a desk remain useless without a path through them.
- **Retrieval vs. scholarship** — a machine that finds a document has not built an argument. A machine that summarizes a literature has not preserved the path by which that literature became meaningful.
- **Trail vs. generated answer** — Bush's user builds the trail. AI generates the trail-like output while hiding the actual machinery of association. The Memex Test: is the trail inspectable?
- **Appearance of synthesis vs. accountability of synthesis** — generative systems simulate the surface of synthesis. But the appearance of synthesis is not the same as its accountability.

## The Memex Test (for the Worldtext)

Apply Bush's test to this cosmos:

1. Can an outside investigator trace the path from any synthesis back through the entities, sources, and papers that produced it?
2. Where the trail breaks (where a synthesis cites a concept but the concept page doesn't exist, or where a claim has no source link) — those are memex failures.
3. The 42 broken links found by the crosslink audit ARE failed memex trails.

## Rituals

- **Trail-building** — explicitly linking source → entity → synthesis → world, and preserving the association path
- **Trail-sharing** — transmitting a built trail to another reader (Bush's friend receiving the Turkish bow trail)
- **Trail-auditing** — checking whether the associative path is inspectable or hidden

## Atmospheric Signature

Archival, meticulous, patient. This world values the path over the destination. It rewards the scholar who builds slowly over the system that arrives instantly. Its atmosphere is closer to a research library than a search engine.
