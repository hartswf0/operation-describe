## Ontology

This is the world where the classical distinction between **describing an image** and **producing an image** collapses. Hannes Bajohr (2024) names this collapse **operative ekphrasis**: in multimodal AI systems, text and image are encoded as the same type of information. The prompt does not merely describe a wished-for image — it *operates* on the latent space to generate one. The ekphrastic act is no longer representational but performative. The word does not stand outside the image; it is computationally entangled with it.

This world inherits the ancient tension of the paragone but transforms it. The word no longer *competes* with the image — it **becomes** the mechanism of image production. The agon does not vanish; it implodes.

The Bolter-Watson paper "From Imagetext to Worldtext" (2026) develops this claim in two directions: (1) the prompt + generated image together constitute a [[concept-imagetext]] in Mitchell's sense — a merged verbal-visual object; (2) as AI moves from images to interactive 3D environments, the imagetext becomes a [[concept-worldtext]] — an interactive, spatio-temporal environment specified in language.

### The Four Innovations

1. **The description produces rather than responds.** No image precedes the prompt. The classical asymmetry (image → description) is reversed (description → image). But the AI mediates, and the [[concept-latent-space]] the prompt navigates is saturated with prior visual culture.

2. **[[concept-abundance]].** AI generates not one image but a multiplicity. The ekphrast samples from a distribution. This challenges authorship, uniqueness, and aura.

3. **The cybernetic loop.** Operative ekphrasis is a feedback loop: prompt → image → evaluation → revised prompt → new image. The prompter is a cybernetic steersman; the latent space is the ocean; the prompt is the rudder.

4. **[[concept-seamless-vs-exposed|The two-path fork]].** The seamless path (photorealism, hidden mediation, ekphrastic hope) vs. the exposed path (multiplicity foregrounded, process visible, ekphrastic fear as aesthetic). Watson's ACHILLES // OPTICAL_GRID_v10 is the primary artifact of the exposed path.

### The Platform Realism Challenge

[[entity-roland-meyer]]'s [[concept-platform-realism]] provides the critical counterpart: prompts "find" images rather than "produce" them — they navigate a latent space shaped by platform capitalism. The space defaults to stock photography, nostalgic vibes, and generic content. This creates the central tension: [[conflict-abundance-vs-platform-realism]].

### The Photorealism Problem

Three sources sharpen the seamless path's failure:
- [[entity-hito-steyerl]]: AI images are "mean images" — statistical averages, "hallucinated mediocrity"
- [[entity-amanda-wasielewski]]: Indexicality is broken but appearance is preserved — the look of photography without the bond to reality
- [[entity-roland-meyer]]: The [[concept-synthetic-camera]] has no lens — it produces "images of possible images"

## Conflict Grammars

- [[conflict-description-vs-generation]] — The world's central tension. Does it fully resolve? Bajohr argues yes, within the connectionist regime. But the human prompter still *intends* description even as the machine *performs* generation.
- [[conflict-human-vs-machine-authorship]] — The generated image has no single author. The prompter provides intent; the model provides materialization; the training data provides the aesthetic substrate.
- [[conflict-abundance-vs-platform-realism]] — The central aesthetic-political conflict: is AI overproduction creative richness (abundance) or visual pollution (platform realism)? Two readings of the same phenomenon.

## Memory Traces

- The paragone tradition persists as a theoretical ghost — scholars still invoke word/image competition even after it has technically collapsed.
- Heffernan's definition ("verbal representation of visual representation") survives as a negative reference — the thing operative ekphrasis moves *beyond*.
- The uncanny valley of early AI art (2015–2022) persists as an atmospheric residue.

## Time Structure

**Eruptive / Ongoing**: This world emerged suddenly with DALL·E (2021) and Stable Diffusion (2022), but its theoretical prefiguration goes back to Bajohr 2024 and earlier intermediality theory. It is still actively forming. The cosmos has not yet stabilized what this world means.

## Variant Forms

- **[[concept-reactive-ekphrasis]]** — Chained text→image→text→image loops. When autonomous, they converge toward 12 generic motifs ("visual elevator music" — Hintze et al. 2025). The human may be constitutive: without the steersman, the loop collapses to default platform taste.

- **[[concept-thick-prompting]]** — The practical dimension: enriching prompts through progressive layers of abstraction and constraint. The T1–T7 framework. See [[thick-prompt]] for the six-layer architecture derived from Geertz.

## Open Questions (from the Overview)

- Does operative ekphrasis fulfil Mitchell's ekphrastic hope, realise his ekphrastic fear, or dissolve the distinction?
- Who is the "silent other" in operative ekphrasis — the model, the absorbed artists, the training data?
- Is the "operative" in operative ekphrasis the same kind of operativity as in Farocki's operative images?
- Who is the ekphrast: the human prompter, the model, or the feedback loop as a whole?
- If images are "found through words" rather than produced (Meyer), is operative ekphrasis a misnomer?
- Does the two-path fork require the paper to take a normative position?
- Is the worldtext concept fully separable from the imagetext concept?
- If autonomous reactive loops converge, what exactly is the human contribution that preserves difference?

---

*This world is the newest and most volatile in the cosmos. It is where the ancient paragone finally implodes — and where a new agon forms: abundance against platform realism, seamless against exposed, production against navigation. The word no longer competes with the image. It competes with itself — with its own defaults, its own convergences, its own mean.*
