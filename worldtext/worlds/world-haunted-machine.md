## Ontology

A world where the machine is always haunted. Calvino's "Cybernetics and Ghosts" makes the claim that literature does not begin as expression — it begins as permutation. The first storyteller of the tribe was not a genius pouring inner self into speech. He was an operator testing what a finite symbolic system could do. Storytelling is combinatorial: a few animals, a few kinship positions, a few actions, a few objects are enough to generate a world.

But the machine is never merely a machine. Somewhere in the combinatorial operation, a remainder appears that the system cannot explain: affect, desire, memory, wound, that specific chill when a story becomes more than its parts. Calvino calls this the ghost.

The central question of this world: **why does literature produce more than its machinery can explain?**

## Places

- **The tribal fire** — Calvino's primitive scene: the storyteller operating a finite symbolic inventory, discovering what the system can do
- **The Oulipo workshop** — constrained writing as experimental proof that restriction is generative
- **The latent space as combinatorial field** — the contemporary version of Calvino's machine: billions of parameters, finite operations, unpredictable remainder
- **The uncanny output** — the moment when the AI image or text produces something that exceeds what the operator intended

## Key Entities

- **Italo Calvino** — "Cybernetics and Ghosts" (1967): literature is machinery plus ghosts
- **Vladimir Propp** — "Morphology of the Folktale": narrative as a finite set of functions (departure, prohibition, violation, struggle, return, recognition)
- **Claude Lévi-Strauss** — myth as transformation system: stories process contradictions that cannot be resolved directly
- **Roland Barthes** — the death of the author: authorship is procedural, not sovereign
- **The Proppian function** — the unit of narrative is not the symbol, the character, or the moral, but the function: an action defined by its place in a sequence

## Conflict Grammars

- **Machinery vs. ghosts** — if you say literature is only structure, it becomes dead machinery. If you say it is only mystery, you ignore the labor of form. Calvino lives in the tension.
- **Combinatorial power vs. affective remainder** — the system generates variation. But some variations wound, charm, terrify, or return as memory. The ghost is the difference between the generated and the felt.
- **Author as origin vs. author as operator** — the romantic model says the author creates ex nihilo. Calvino says the author activates latent possibility within a symbolic field. AI makes this visible: the prompter is Calvino's storyteller, not Shelley's Prometheus.
- **Training data as cultural memory** — Lévi-Strauss's transformation system IS what training data encodes: genre conventions, mythic oppositions, plot expectations, ideological structures. The model generates because culture was always patterned.

## Rituals

- **Combinatorial testing** — running the same material through different arrangements to see what the system can do
- **Ghost-detection** — reading AI output for the affective remainder: what did this generate that no one asked for?
- **The archive as transformation engine** — treating the dataset not as a storage box but as a system of possible substitutions

## Atmospheric Signature

Playful, precise, slightly uncanny. This world has the discipline of structuralism and the unease of the supernatural. Its atmosphere is closer to a well-lit laboratory where something unexplainable just happened.
