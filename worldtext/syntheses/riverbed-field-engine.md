# The Riverbed Field Engine

> A hydrologic space-time compiler. Separates what stands fast from what moves.

---

## Core Thesis

Every source — an idea, a passage, an image, a scene, a code system, a world, a prompt — is a **river system**.

The Riverbed Field Engine compiles any input into an **operative field** by identifying six fundamental dynamics:

| Element | Definition | Role |
|---------|-----------|------|
| **Riverbed** | The stable identity-structure that must stand fast for the source to remain itself | Constraint |
| **Water** | The moving variation that flows through the riverbed without breaking identity | Motion |
| **Sediment** | Residue left by motion: memory, trace, habit, artifact, scar, convention, deposit | Trace |
| **Erosion** | Repeated flow changing the riverbed: failure, pressure, use, drift, accident, friction, recurrence carving a new channel | Transformation |
| **Accretion** | Residue hardening into structure: motif becoming law, habit becoming interface, trace becoming architecture, repeated gesture becoming rule | Stabilization |
| **Mud** | Unresolved mixture of motion, memory, atmosphere, and constraint | Ambiguity |

---

## The Core Law

```
SPACE = constraint topology
        zones, surfaces, distances, thresholds, paths,
        occlusions, interfaces, access rules, material resistance

TIME  = transformation topology
        moments, durations, intervals, rhythms, delays,
        recurrences, interruptions, traces, anticipations,
        returns, decay, acceleration

SPACE-TIME = a navigable field where every place carries duration
             and every duration occupies a place
```

## The Compilation Method

### Step 1: Read from the bottom up
Do not begin with the title, the thesis, or the abstract. Begin with the material ground — the lowest, most concrete layer of the source. What is physically present? What actually moves?

### Step 2: Identify the field
What kind of space-time system does the source imply? Is it a room? A city? A conversation? A prompt-response cycle? A research project? A body?

### Step 3: Separate riverbed from water
What must remain constant for the source to remain itself? What is free to vary without destroying identity?

### Step 4: Identify what erodes
What is changing the riverbed? What repeated forces, pressures, or failures are carving new channels in the identity-structure?

### Step 5: Identify what accretes
What residue is hardening into new structure? What motifs are becoming laws? What habits are becoming interfaces? What traces are becoming architecture?

### Step 6: Identify where mud appears
Where is the mixture unresolved? Where is motion tangled with memory? Where is atmosphere obscuring structure? Where is constraint fertilizing growth?

### Step 7–12: Convert all abstractions into operations

| From | To |
|------|----|
| **Nouns** | zones, objects, structures, traces, agents, thresholds, artifacts, temporal carriers |
| **Verbs** | forces, transitions, rhythms, repetitions, delays, accelerations, interruptions, returns, erosions, accretions, transformations |
| **Adjectives** | light, texture, pressure, density, tempo, scale, temperature, frequency, persistence, volatility, atmosphere |
| **Emotions** | climate and temporal tension: dread, suspension, urgency, waiting, recurrence, aftermath, release, anticipation |
| **Relations** | thresholds, bridges, gradients, interfaces, distances, overlaps, lags, synchronizations, before/after/during/between/return |

---

## The Ban List

Do not produce:

- Vague summary
- Generic cinematic adjectives
- Idle abstraction
- Equal weighting of critical and trivial details
- Psychology unless materially visible
- Symbolism without physical carrier
- "maybe," "perhaps," "it seems," "dreamlike," "surreal," "essence," "journey," "alchemy"
- Prompt clutter that does not change generation
- Water that floods the riverbed
- Riverbed stated as optional detail

## Application to Worldtext Operations

The Riverbed Field Engine maps directly onto the worldtext architecture:

| Riverbed Concept | Worldtext Equivalent |
|-----------------|---------------------|
| Riverbed | World Bible — invariant design-time constraints |
| Water | Lore Book — subjective, contradictory, mythic variation |
| Sediment | Encyclopedia — indexed traces of past motion |
| Erosion | Continuity Debt — repeated generation without repair |
| Accretion | Canonization — motif hardening into law |
| Mud | Unresolved mixture awaiting operator judgment |

### The Key Insight

The distinction between riverbed and water is the **same** distinction as between World Bible and Lore Book. The Riverbed Field Engine provides a universal compilation method for any source material, while the worldtext architecture provides the persistent storage and retrieval infrastructure.

---

## Mud as Resource

> Keep it clear enough to steer and muddy enough to grow.

Mud is the most operationally subtle element. It is:

- **Dangerous** when it obscures control — when the operator cannot tell whether something is riverbed or water.
- **Productive** when it fertilizes new structure — when unresolved mixture generates novel combinations that neither pure constraint nor pure variation could produce.

The operator's discipline is **mud literacy**: knowing when to clarify and when to let the mixture do its work.
