## The Hidden Connection

Fjellestad (2018) introduces the concept of **assemblage** to ekphrasis studies — borrowed from Deleuze/Guattari via DeLanda — to describe what happens when a novel contains not one ekphrastic moment but many, and those moments don't follow any "single organizing principle."

> "An object (image) can be taken out of a given ensemble, and transferred to another, its meaning emerging through a new set of relationships." — Fjellestad 2018, p. 340

This concept — **relational constellation without fixed hierarchy** — is structurally identical to:

1. **Voronoi tessellation** in the Double Barrel visualization: territories emerge from proximity, not from pre-assigned categories. The shape of a galaxy is an emergent property of its contents' spatial relationships.

2. **The WorldText cosmos itself**: 993 entries organized not by a taxonomy imposed from above but by **reference density** — things that cite each other cluster together. The galaxies are assemblages.

3. **CLIP's embedding space** (Bajohr 2024): images and texts are not sorted into categories but mapped to positions in a continuous vector space. Proximity = meaning.

## What This Means

The WorldText cosmos is already an assemblage in Fjellestad's sense. But Fjellestad identifies three provenances of the concept:

| Provenance | Definition | Cosmos Equivalent |
|-----------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Archaeology** | artifacts found in association with one another | Evidence entries sharing a Zotero collection |
| **DeLanda/Deleuze** | temporary configurations of heterogeneous entities | Wiki-link networks between WorldText pages |
| **Art criticism (MoMA 1961)** | composite art between collage and construction | The Voronoi canvas itself |

The insight: **the Double Barrel visualization is not a representation of the cosmos — it IS the cosmos performing assemblage.** Each Voronoi cell is an artifact found in association. Each drill-down is a reterritorialization (Deleuze). Each wiki-link is a line of flight.

## Jansson's Simultaneous/Consecutive Problem

Jansson (2018) identifies a paradox in digital ekphrasis: text and image appear **simultaneously AND consecutively** — simultaneously on screen, consecutively in production. This breaks Varga's binary.

This exact problem maps to the Double Barrel split-view:
- **Voronoi view** = simultaneous (all territories visible at once, spatial)
- **Sediment view** = consecutive (layers stacked in time, temporal)
- **The user navigates both at once** → simultaneous AND consecutive

The Split View is the formal solution to Jansson's word/image copresence problem: you can see the same data both ways at once, and the navigation is synchronized.

## The Yacobi Conjecture

Yacobi (2013, via Jansson and Fjellestad) argues that when the image is copresent with the text, "the poet can turn the image into 'a point of departure'" — the poem is free to go anywhere because the image is already there.

**In the cosmos**: when the evidence (the Zotero entry) is copresent with the world page (the synthesis), the world page is free to *interpret* rather than *describe*. The full-text extraction we just did makes this literal: now that every source is searchable text alongside its WorldText summary, the summaries are free to think rather than transcribe.

## Toward Assemblage Ekphrasis

The non-obvious conclusion: **the WorldText cosmos is a text that does what Fjellestad describes novels doing — it is a "conglomerate of original and appropriated texts" that assembles them without a single organizing principle.** The galaxies are not taxonomies; they are assemblage zones. The Voronoi cells are not categories; they are territories of proximity.

This makes the cosmos itself an ekphrastic act: a verbal representation of a knowledge structure that does not yet have a visual form — until the Double Barrel gives it one. The cosmos is the code that produces the image. It is operative ekphrasis of the second kind: text performing visualization without describing a pre-existing picture.
