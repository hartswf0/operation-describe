- Richly written world pages that create the *atmosphere* of depth — but rely on ~30 unique sources.
- Affects: HIGH. This is the most dangerous kind of dark matter: the kind that passes casual inspection.

### Cherry-Pick Test

> *Are only favorable cases presented?*

**Verdict**: **YES.** The cosmos was compiled from the easiest-to-process top of the evidence pyramid:
- The 85 formally-typed entries (books, journal articles, conference papers) with real abstracts were used.
- The 623 document-type entries (web captures, often with placeholder authors) were effectively ignored.
- The world pages cite the canonical names (Mitchell, Heffernan, Geertz, Bajohr) and ignore the 280+ other real authors in the corpus.
- No world page engages with the large clusters of technical AI papers, HCI studies, or design research — the hardest material to compile into worlds.

### Source Integrity Test

> *Do citations look appropriate and specific?*

**Verdict**: **PARTIALLY.** The evidence bases listed on world pages are real and correctly attributed. However:
- No source is individually processed per COSMIC_LAW requirements.
- The "~80 entries," "~150 entries" cluster estimates in the source summary are unverifiable gestures.
- Bajohr 2024 is cited as the linchpin of the cosmos (operative ekphrasis as event horizon), but it is a single article. The entire world-type `operative-ekphrasis-world` and the system `system-operative-ekphrasis` rest on this single source plus a handful of digital poetics articles.

### Safety / Ethics Test

> *Does the work minimize potential harms?*

**Verdict**: **Not directly applicable** (this is a knowledge system, not a deployed product). However, the cosmos claims 50+ entries on "AI Ethics, Governance, Society" but routes **zero** of those into dedicated governance or ethics pages. The `governance/` directory is empty. ### Guardian Justification

- **Why the grade cannot be higher:**
  1. **93.6% of sources are unprocessed.** The cosmos claims to be compiled from 708 sources but was actually compiled from ~45. This is the most severe deficiency: the evidence stratum is a ghost town.
  2. **82.7% of cross-links are broken.** The cosmos simulates interlinking through wiki-style references but 91 of 110 references point to non-existent pages. 16 directories are empty scaffolding.
  3. **World-type discovery criteria are not met.** At least 3 of 10 world-types fail the stated ≥3 source threshold. The taxonomy is built from analogy and pattern-recognition rather than from verified evidence accumulation.
  4. **Anti-patterns are present.** "Taxonomies without evidence" and "atmospheres without structures" — two of the cosmos's own prohibited anti-patterns — are active in the current state.

- **Where the text is strongest:**
  1. **The 10 world pages are genuinely excellent.** They have clear ontologies, structural completeness, and real intellectual depth. `world-classical-ekphrasis.md` and `world-operative-ekphrasis.md` in particular are the best pages in the cosmos.
  2. **The cosmological architecture is sound.** The galaxy → system → world → place hierarchy is logically coherent and the symbolic distinctions that organize it are well-chosen.
  3. **Contradictions and frontier questions are honest.** The cosmos identifies its own gaps (missing atmospheres, thin AI worlds, unresolved definition wars).

- **Most dangerous dark matter if ignored:**
  1. The 663 unprocessed sources. If the cosmos grows without processing these, it will increasingly resemble an echo chamber of the same ~45 sources.
  2. The phantom interlink structure. Users/agents navigating via `[[links]]` will hit 91 dead ends, creating the appearance of a rich knowledge graph that is actually a stub graph.
  3. The 466 placeholder-author entries. The corpus may contain significant duplicates, misattributions, or non-sources that would alter the cosmos if audited.
