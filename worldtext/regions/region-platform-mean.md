# Region: The Platform Mean

> **Species**: region  
> **Scale**: region  
> **Parent World**: [[world-latent-space]]

---

## Definition

The **platform mean** is the region at the center of the latent space's statistical distribution — the area where generations converge when prompt pressure is low or absent. It is the default aesthetic of the training corpus: stock photography, golden hour, clean surfaces, nostalgic vibes, photorealistic skin, "cinematic lighting."

## Topology

The platform mean is **dense** — heavily populated with training examples, producing high-confidence outputs. The model's gradient landscape is smooth here: small prompt changes produce small output changes. Viscosity is high: the distribution resists perturbation. All roads lead back to this region unless active force is applied.

Hintze et al.'s autonomous loops (reactive ekphrasis) converge to this region's 12 dominant motifs: "visual elevator music." The lighthouses, the coastal sunsets, the headshots with gaussian-blurred backgrounds.

## The Region's Edges

At the edges of the platform mean, the topology becomes rougher:
- **Low-probability regions**: Unusual concept combinations, rare aesthetic styles, non-Western visual conventions
- **Out-of-distribution regions**: Concept collisions (e.g., "a brick made of water"), hallucinatory conjunctions, creative rupture
- **Attractor basins**: Named artists, specific cultural movements, identifiable photographers whose work clusters in the training data (e.g., "Lotte Reiniger" as an attractor basin for shadow-puppet aesthetics)

The thick prompter's skill is **navigating away from the platform mean toward a specific attractor basin or low-probability region** without falling into incoherence.
