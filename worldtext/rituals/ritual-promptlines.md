## Definition

Promptlines are recorded sequences of prompt revisions that trace the operator's path from initial attempt through iterative refinement to final output. They are the [[world-memex-trail]] applied to prompt-based generation: a durable, inspectable, transmissible trail through the latent space.

## Structure

A promptline consists of:
1. **Initial prompt** — the first attempt
2. **Output** — what the model returned
3. **Diagnosis** — what was wrong, unexpected, or revealing
4. **Revised prompt** — the next attempt, informed by the diagnosis
5. **Repeat** until convergence, abandonment, or surprising discovery
6. **Terminal note** — what the operator learned that could not have been learned without the iteration

## Why It Matters

Promptlines solve the memex problem for AI-assisted practice. Without them, prompt → output interactions are ephemeral. The operator generates, selects, and forgets the path. With promptlines, the path becomes a scholarly artifact — inspectable, teachable, citable, criticizable.

They also solve the argue.json finding that "the argument is not the thought." The promptline IS the thought — the groping, the wrong turns, the revisions that the finished output conceals.
