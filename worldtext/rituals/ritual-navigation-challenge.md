## Definition

The navigation challenge is a deliberate prompt exercise where the operator attempts to reach a specific aesthetic target in the latent space through iterative prompting. Unlike casual generation (prompt → accept/reject), the navigation challenge requires the operator to maintain a sustained intention across multiple attempts, each one informed by the failure of the previous.

## Structure

1. **Set the target** — a specific image the operator can describe but cannot yet generate
2. **First attempt** — prompt the model; observe the gap between intention and output
3. **Diagnose** — identify what the model defaulted to (platform realism) and what the operator failed to specify
4. **Revise** — thicken the prompt; add layers, constraints, anti-instructions
5. **Iterate** — repeat until the output converges with the intention or the operator discovers their intention was wrong
6. **Log** — record the prompt sequence as a trail (a memex trail through the latent space)

## Why It Matters

The navigation challenge transforms prompting from command-execution (thin) to exploration-through-resistance (thick). It is the [[world-latent-wrestling]] version of [[world-descriptive-struggle]]: the operator struggles to describe what they want, and the struggle IS the thinking.
