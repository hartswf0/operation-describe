## Definition

A prompt battle is a competitive or comparative exercise where two or more operators attempt to generate the most aesthetically, conceptually, or technically adequate output from the same base condition (same model, same subject, sometimes same seed). The battle reveals that prompting is not neutral command-giving but a skilled practice with measurable variation.

## Structure

1. **Shared constraint** — all participants use the same model, same subject, same time limit
2. **Independent prompting** — each operator writes their own prompt(s), revise as needed
3. **Comparison** — outputs are laid side by side, evaluated by agreed criteria or blind judging
4. **Discussion** — the prompts are revealed, and the group analyzes why certain prompts produced better contact with the target

## Why It Matters

The prompt battle is the empirical test of [[concept-thick-prompting]]. If a thick prompt consistently outperforms a thin prompt, thick prompting is real. If results are random, the concept is empty.
