## Definition

The **AUDIT** is the fourth verb of worldtext operations — the missing verb that completes the INGEST → QUERY → LINT → AUDIT cycle. Where LINT inspects the cosmos for structural breakdown, AUDIT inspects the **operator** for cognitive drift.

## Protocol

Run quarterly. Answer the following questions:

1. **Query Drift**: What did I query last month that I never query now?
2. **Vocabulary Gain**: What new terms have I introduced to my operative vocabulary?
3. **Vocabulary Loss**: What terms have I stopped using? Were they deliberately retired or silently dropped?
4. **Source Bias**: What sources do I always deploy? What sources do I avoid?
5. **Constraint Range**: Am I generating with the same five constraints or exploring new ones?
6. **Tool Dependency**: Which diagnostic programs do I run frequently? Which have I never run?
7. **Counter-Reading Gap**: Do my syntheses contain counter-readings, or are they self-confirming?

## Output

File results as a dated entry in `worldtext/syntheses/` with species `audit-report`. Append a summary to `chronicle.md`.

## Why It Exists

Without AUDIT, the operator becomes a [[entity-fragile-expert|Fragile Expert]]: confident in output quality, shrinking in range, unable to detect their own ossification. The [[entity-viscosity-trap|Viscosity Trap]] operates through the operator. AUDIT is the second-order observation that makes the trap visible.
