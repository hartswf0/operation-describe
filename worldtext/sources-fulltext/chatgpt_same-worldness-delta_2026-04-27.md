2. CORE FIELD PREMISE
A generative world does not become inhabitable when a model produces scenes; it becomes inhabitable when separate images, texts, maps, diagrams, interfaces, rooms, prompts, and artifacts remain recognizable as exposures of the same hidden order across time.

4. TIME LOGIC
The field moves through repeated exposure cycles:

Seed Event: a prompt, bible, concept map, image, artifact, or service desire enters the system.

AI world-model teams now sell the oldest fantasy in computing: type a prompt, get a world.

However, their demos keep exposing the same failure.

They generate places. They do not generate worlds.

A room is not a world. A corridor is not a world. A thousand pretty scenes are not a world. They become a world only when the next thing must answer to the last thing.

That is the missing discipline.

The Shield of Achilles already solved the problem that AI world models keep dodging. Hephaestus does not make “content.” He builds order. That is not world generation.

The product problem is brutal: users do not want to become unpaid world janitors. Game studios know this. Simulation teams know this. Service providers know this. A world must hold before the user enters.

Imagetext handles the bond between image and language.

Worldtext handles the harder bond between many artifacts and one world-order.

A caption can make imagetext.

A shield, a map, a court, a battlefield, a vineyard, a dance, and an ocean rim working under one order make worldtext.

Order is now the scarce thing.
