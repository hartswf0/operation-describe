## The Discovery

Han et al. (2024) find that teams co-prompting GenAI alternate between two strategies:
1. **Story-first**: describe the narrative → generate visuals (verbal → visual)
2. **Visual-first**: generate imagery → construct narrative (visual → verbal)

These two strategies **are** the two classical directions of ekphrasis:
1. **Descriptive ekphrasis** (Theon, Aphthonius): from words to mental image = story-first
2. **Responsive ekphrasis** (Philostratus's *Imagines*): from viewing to verbal response = visual-first

| Han et al. Strategy | Classical Ekphrastic Mode | Mechanism |
|---------------------|--------------------------|-----------|
| Story-first | Descriptive (progymnasmata) | Verbal specification generates visual output |
| Visual-first | Responsive (Imagines) | Visual stimulus provokes verbal elaboration |
| Alternation between modes | Paragone oscillation | The word/image competition as generative engine |

## The Deeper Connections

### 1. The Team as Audience
Webb (2016) emphasizes that ancient ekphrasis assumed **live interaction between speaker and audience**. Han's teams replicate this social structure: the prompt is written by one person, interpreted by the AI, then evaluated by the team. The team IS the classical audience — judging the enargeia of the prompt by what it produces.

### 2. Boundary Objects = Pictorial Third
Han et al. find that generated images serve as **"boundary objects"** — shared reference points that enable teams with different expertise to communicate and reach consensus. This IS Louvel's pictorial third: an emergent artifact that exists in the gap between verbal intention and visual realization, belonging to neither producer alone.

### 3. Tool Perception = Paragone
The finding that teams perceive GenAI as a "tool" rather than a "collaborator" maps onto the paragone tradition: the painting/sculpture was always the subordinate medium that served the verbal master's purpose. The AI, like the painting, is acted UPON by words. It does not act back.

But Han's finding that strong human collaboration **reduces** AI reliance suggests the deeper truth: the paragone was never about word vs. image but about **human communication seeking the most efficient channel**. When humans communicate well verbally, they need less visual mediation.

### 4. Live Performance = Iterative Prompting
The ancient rhetor performed ekphrasis before an audience in real time, adjusting based on audience response (nods, gasps, shifting attention). Han's teams adjust their prompts based on AI output in real time. Both are **iterative, live performance** — not static text production.
