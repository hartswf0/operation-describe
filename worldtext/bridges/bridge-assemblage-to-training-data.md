## The Parallel

Fjellestad (2018) borrows **assemblage** from Deleuze/DeLanda to describe novels containing multiple ekphrastic moments without a "single organizing principle." The images are not sequenced by narrative logic; they are *assembled* — brought into proximity without an author's controlling hand.

LAION-5B, the training dataset for Stable Diffusion, contains **5.85 billion image-text pairs** scraped from the web with no curatorial intent. The pairs are not selected by theme, quality, artist, or ideology. They are *assembled* — accumulated through automated web scraping.

Both produce meaning through the same mechanism: **statistical proximity**. Things that appear together enough times acquire a relationship — even if no one intended that relationship.

## The Five Properties of Assemblage (DeLanda)

| Property | Fjellestad's Novel | AI Training Data |
|----------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **Heterogeneity** | Mixed genres: captions, letters, photographs, prices | Mixed media: photos, paintings, diagrams, memes, ads |
| **Territorialization** | The novel binds the images within its covers | The training pipeline binds the data within a statistical model |
| **Deterritorialization** | Each image can be extracted and re-contextualized | Each image-text pair can be sampled, augmented, decontextualized |
| **Coding** | The novel's genre (auction catalogue) gives the assemblage a legible frame | The loss function (contrastive learning) gives the assemblage a mathematical frame |
| **Material agency** | Images have material properties (size, position, reproduction quality) | Data has computational properties (resolution, format, encoding) |

## What This Means for Authorship

Both assemblages raise the same question: **who is the author?**

- In Fjellestad's analysis: Shapton's *Important Artifacts* presents objects as though curated by an auctioneer, not narrated by a novelist. The "author" is a curatorial function, not a creative voice.
- In AI training: the "author" of LAION-5B is a scraping algorithm, not a human curator. The "author" of the model's visual vocabulary is the aggregate of billions of anonymous web users who posted the images.

In both cases, **meaning emerges without authorial intent** — from the density of associations between heterogeneous elements. This is Derrida's *il n'y a pas de hors-texte* made literal: there is no outside to the training dataset, because the model's entire visual reality IS the dataset.

## The Ethical Dimension

Fjellestad's assemblage is ethically neutral (Shapton assembled auction photos). But AI training data assemblages are ethically charged:
- Unconsented scraping of artists' work
- Reproduction of biases embedded in web-scale data
- The curator (scraping algorithm) has no aesthetic judgment

Kennedy's (2018) distinction between "good" and "bad" intermediality applies directly:
- **"Good" assemblage**: heterogeneous materials brought together to produce new meaning (Fjellestad's novels, well-curated training data)
- **"Bad" assemblage**: heterogeneous materials thrown together without care, reproducing existing power structures (web scraping without consent)
