## Citation

Bajohr, Hannes. "Operative ekphrasis: the collapse of the text/image distinction in multimodal AI." *Word & Image* 40, no. 2 (2024). DOI: 10.1080/02666286.2024.2330335

## Core Argument

Bajohr proposes a tripartite taxonomy of text/image relationships based on their technical substrate:

1. **Analogue** (typewriter, manual): text "creates" image only through bodily arrangement — no operative ekphrasis
2. **Sequential digital** (classical algorithms, code): a "first text" (code) causally produces a "second text" (image) — **operative ekphrasis type 1** — pragmatics without semantics
3. **Connectionist digital** (neural networks, multimodal AI): text and image are encoded as the same type of information in a shared embedding space — **operative ekphrasis type 2** — semantics without pragmatics

The central thesis: *"Only in the context of multimodal AI, unlike in analogue or sequential paradigms, ekphrasis goes beyond the separation of or transition between text and image, but rather transcends this difference."*

## Key Concepts Defined

### Operative Ekphrasis
> "Understanding ekphrasis not as representation but as performance; not as imitating an image through text, but as text effectively bringing about an image." (p. 83)

This extends Brosch's performative redefinition of ekphrasis into digital media: the code *does* image, it does not *describe* image.

### Sequential vs. Connectionist Paradigms
The crucial subdivision *within* the digital:
- **Sequential**: deterministic, transparent, step-by-step (von Neumann architecture). Code is "syntax without semantics."
- **Connectionist**: stochastic, opaque, parallel (neural networks). Weight models cannot be "read" as instructions.

### Artificial Semantics
> "Neural networks, and multimodal models in particular, may indeed be concerned with something that may not be meaning in the full sense of human communication, but cannot be confidently labelled non-meaning either. This 'dumb' meaning is what I call artificial semantics." (p. 86)

### The CLIP Grandmother Cell
Bajohr argues (via OpenAI's 2021 research) that CLIP develops "grandmother neurons" — single neurons that respond to both visual and textual representations of the same entity (e.g., Spiderman as photo, drawing, and text). This parallels neuroscience hypotheses about concept cells.

## The Chiasmus

| Paradigm | Pragmatics | Semantics | Language |
|----------|:----------:|:---------:|----------|
| Sequential (code) | ✓ (commands execute) | ✗ (syntax only) | "acts without meaning" |
| Connectionist (AI) | ✗ (no command structure) | ✓ (shared representation) | "means without acting" |

This chiasmus is the paper's deepest structural contribution: the performative dimension shifts from the *execution* of code to the *formulation* of the prompt.

## Position in the Paragone

Bajohr explicitly rejects both classical positions:
- **NOT Lessing** (ekphrastic fear — mediums must be separated)
- **NOT Horace** (ekphrastic hope — ut pictura poesis, productive transformation)
- **A third position**: multimodal AI creates a "multimodal pictorial third" (borrowing Louvel's term) — a shared meaning-space beyond word and image
