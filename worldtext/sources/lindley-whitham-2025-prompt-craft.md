## Citation

Lindley, Joseph, and Roger Whitham. "From Prompt Engineering to Prompt Craft." *Pictorial*, 2025.

## Core Argument

Lindley & Whitham argue that the dominant framing of text-to-image interaction as "prompt engineering" (a technical, optimizable process) obscures what is actually happening: a **craft practice** involving embodied knowledge, material negotiation, and iterative refinement.

### Key Claims

1. **Engineering → Craft**: Prompting is not engineering (deterministic, repeatable) but craft (material-responsive, embodied, iterative). The AI system has "metaphorical material properties" that the practitioner learns to negotiate.

2. **Three Practice-Based Projects**: The paper reports on three Stable Diffusion-based design research projects conducted in 2024, each demonstrating different aspects of prompt-as-craft:
   - Tangible and embodied interactions as inquiry
   - The "uncertainty" and "materiality" of generative AI
   - The Yiddish proverb: "Man plans, God laughs" (unpredictability of output)

3. **Research through Design (RtD)**: The methodology itself is the argument — the researchers do not test hypotheses about prompting, they practice prompting and reflect on the practice.

## Position in the Cosmos

This paper operationalizes Bajohr's "operative ekphrasis" as a craft dialogue. Where Bajohr analyzes the structural collapse of text/image, Lindley & Whitham show what it feels like to **work within** that collapse.
