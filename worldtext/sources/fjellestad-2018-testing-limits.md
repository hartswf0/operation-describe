## Citation

Fjellestad, Danuta. "Testing the Limits and Powers of Ekphrasis: Faux Catalog Writing, Assemblage, and Narrativization." *Poetics Today* 39, no. 2 (2018): 337–358.

## Core Argument

When a novel contains not one ekphrastic moment but **many** (reproduced images alongside verbal descriptions), the organizational logic is not narrative sequence or thematic unity but **assemblage**: "the process of verbal aggregation of visual objects brought together into a constellation without any single organizing principle."

### Key Concepts

**Pictured Ekphrasis**: ekphrasis accompanied by a reproduced image (image-plus-word), complementing Yacobi's "picture-less" ekphrasis. This doubles the interruption: the narrative flow is punctuated by *both* description and image.

**Assemblage (triple provenance)**:
1. **Archaeology** — artifacts found in association with one another (shared context without causal link)
2. **Deleuze/DeLanda** — temporary configurations of heterogeneous entities emphasizing materiality and coming-together
3. **Art criticism (MoMA 1961)** — composite art between collage and construction

**Shapton's *Important Artifacts***: A novel masquerading as an auction catalogue — ekphrasis performing its own commercial framing. The "curator" replaces the "narrator." Images are not described but captioned, priced, and inventoried.
