## Abstract / Summary

Focusing on ekphrastic poetry, I argue that James A. W. Heffernan’s succint definition of ekphrasis as the “verbal representation of a visual representation”, much cited by others and still upheld in his essay “Ekphrasis: Theory” in the 2015 Handbook of Intermediality (org. G. Rippl), has become even less tenable than when I criticized it in 1995. I then showed that it excluded ekphrastic descriptions of non-representational painting and sculpture as well as all architecture. I am now supported by David Kennedy’s recent studies, who has shown that contemporary critical ekphrasis and the surrounding discourse have turned against the model of a paragone based on representation.
