## Core Argument

Auden's 1952 poem "The Shield of Achilles" makes a critical change to Homer's Iliad XVIII: he introduces **Thetis as a viewer** of the shield while Hephaestus makes it. In Homer, Thetis is left outside the workshop ("he left her there," Il. 18.468). Auden brings her in, and her viewing — her disappointment at what she sees — becomes the poem's central drama.

### The Viewing Chain

This creates a three-level chain:
1. **Hephaestus** makes the scenes (the creator / the model)
2. **Thetis** views the making and is horrified (the audience / the evaluator)
3. **The reader** reads Auden's poem describing what Thetis views (the ekphrastic reader / us)

This is ekphrasis-at-three-removes: a text describing a viewer watching a maker making images. The reader sees Auden's words → which describe Thetis seeing → which describes Hephaestus making → which depicts scenes of war and peace.

### Auden's Dystopian Inversion

Where Homer's shield shows a complete world (weddings, harvests, dances, law courts, sieges), Auden's shield shows a devastated modernity:
- Barbed-wire fences replace pastoral fields
- "An unintelligible multitude" replaces the singing procession
- Three ragged urchins replace the dancing youths

Thetis "looked over his shoulder / For vines and olive trees, / Marble well-governed cities / And ships upon untamed seas." She expected beauty; she got reality.

## THE BRIDGE: Thetis = The User Viewing AI Output

Thetis's experience is **structurally identical** to the experience of a user evaluating AI-generated images:

| Thetis (Homer/Auden) | User (TTI Generation) |
|----------------------|-----------------------|
| She commissions the shield (asks Hephaestus to make armor for Achilles) | The user writes a prompt (asks the model to generate an image) |
| She has expectations ("vines and olive trees") | The user has expectations (mental image of what the prompt should produce) |
| Hephaestus makes something different from her expectations | The model generates something different from the user's mental image |
| She looks over his shoulder while he works | The user watches the generation progress bar / diffusion steps |
| Her response is disappointment: the shield shows reality, not her wish | The user's response can be disappointment, surprise, or unexpected discovery |
| She has no power to change what Hephaestus makes | The user has limited power to change what the model generates (can re-prompt) |

### The Evaluator's Dilemma

Thetis embodies what Kafalenos calls the **perspectival montage**: her perception is superimposed on Hephaestus's creation. She "misrepresents" the shield by reading it through her hopes. Auden's reader then adds a third layer — we misrepresent Thetis's misrepresentation.

In the AI context: the user evaluates the generated image through their own expectations, which may differ from what the model intended (its training distribution). The evaluation is always a perspectival montage of user-intent and model-output.
