## Citation

## What This Source Says

Wasielewski brings photography theory to bear on AI-generated images, arguing for continuity rather than rupture:

1. **AI photographs ARE photographs** — not because of their process but because photographic *form* (visual qualities associated with photography) is how they function culturally. "Photographic verisimilitude, not indexicality, is the root of the illusion."

2. **Indexicality broken, appearance preserved**: Classical photography's causal bond with reality (Pierce's index, Krieger's "natural sign") is absent from AI images. Yet the aesthetic surface — the *look* of indexicality — survives intact. What the naive prompter pursuing photorealism gets is indexical appearance without indexical substance.

3. **Baudrillard's hyperreal**: "Generation by models of a real without origin or reality." AI images are the fulfillment of Baudrillard — images that precede their referents, reality produced from the code rather than coded from reality.

4. **AI glitches as modified punctum**: Where Barthes' punctum is material contingency (the accident in the photograph), AI glitches are *statistical* contingency — unpredictable artifacts of the generation process. A modified punctum: it pierces, but the wound comes from mathematics, not from reality.

5. **Galton/Sekula genealogy**: Rooted in Foucauldian power critique, Wasielewski traces the composite photograph from Galton (racial typing) to Sekula (police identification) to AI (statistical averaging). The eugenics and surveillance dimension deepens Meyer's and Steyerl's accounts.

6. **Operative images connection**: AI images as the fulfillment of [[entity-harun-farocki]]'s trajectory — photography's objectification of vision, now automated. Images that do not show the world but act on it.
