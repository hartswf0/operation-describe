## Citation

Elleström, Lars. *Media Borders, Multimodality and Intermediality*. Basingstoke: Palgrave Macmillan, 2010.

## Core Argument

Elleström develops a systematic taxonomy of media modalities that dissolves the binary of "text vs. image" into a richer dimensional space. Media are characterized by four modalities:

1. **Material** — the physical/virtual substrate
2. **Sensorial** — which senses are engaged (sight, hearing, touch)
3. **Spatiotemporal** — the arrangement in space and/or time
4. **Semiotic** — how meaning is made (iconic, indexical, symbolic)

### Intermediality
Intermediality is not a special condition; it is the **default state** of all media. Every medium involves multiple modalities. What we call "intermediality" is simply the foregrounding of these always-present cross-modal connections.

### Multimodality
Multimodality refers to the co-presence of multiple semiotic modes within a single medium. Digital media are inherently multimodal in ways that analog media only approximate.
