## Citation

Hintze, A., et al. (2025). "Language-Image Loops: Autonomous Multimodal Drift in Text-to-Image and Image-to-Text Chains."

## What This Source Says

Hintze et al. place image generation models and image description models into autonomous loops — prompt → image → description → image → description → ... — and track the trajectories across 700 runs. The key findings:

1. **Convergence**: Across all trajectories, the loops converge toward just **12 generic visual motifs**: lighthouses, cathedrals, palatial interiors, atmospheric city scenes, and similar stock-photo-like forms.

2. **"Visual elevator music"**: The paper's term for the convergent aesthetic field. Without human intervention, multimodal chains produce images that are pleasant, legible, and entirely generic — the visual equivalent of background music in a hotel lobby.

3. **Attractor basins**: The convergence is not random — the images converge to specific attractor basins in the latent space, corresponding to the highest-frequency regions of the training distribution.
