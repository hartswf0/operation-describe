## Citation

Steyerl, Hito. (2023). "Mean Images." *New Left Review*, 140/141.

## What This Source Says

Steyerl argues that AI-generated images are **statistical means** of the training corpus — mean in both senses: (1) average, and (2) mediocre. They are not faithful representations of any specific vision but composites produced by averaging across competing visual conventions.

Key formulations:
- **"Hallucinated mediocrity"**: the AI image is not a hallucination of something real but a hallucination of the average
- **The Galton composite analogy**: like Francis Galton's composite photographs (which averaged faces to produce a racial "type"), diffusion models optimize for the center of the distribution
- **"Likenesses replaced by likelihoods"**: what AI produces is not a likeness (resemblance to something) but a likelihood (a statistically probable configuration)
