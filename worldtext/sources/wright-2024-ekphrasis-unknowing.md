## Citation

Wright, Mark. "Ekphrasis and the Art of Unknowing." *Writing in Practice* 10 (2024): 98–115.

## Core Argument

Poets responding to **abstract art** (Rothko, Pollock, De Kooning) tend to *evade* the image — writing about the artist's biography, the gallery space, their own feelings — rather than staying with the image itself. Wright proposes an **"unknowing" approach**: deliberately suspending prior knowledge, aesthetic judgment, and interpretive frameworks to encounter the image freshly.

### Key Concepts

**Unknowing** (via Wilfred Bion): "the capacity to forget, the ability to eschew desire and understanding." The psychoanalyst listens without assuming meaning; the ekphrastic poet looks without assuming significance. Not ignorance but **deliberate openness**.

**Ekphrastic Inheritance** (via Hetherington & Atherton 2023): when a poet writes about Rothko *knowing* all the previous Rothko poems. This "knowing" produces cliché and recurrence. Unknowing is the antidote.

**Encounter over Rivalry** (via Kennedy 2012, Guthrie 2013): ekphrasis as "an interaction that doesn't require a winner... It revels in the image, even if the artwork is ironic, a spectacle. It asks, it reveals, it wonders."

## THE BRIDGE: Unknowing = Diffusion Noise

Wright's "unknowing" is structurally identical to the **initial noise** in a diffusion model's denoising process:

| Unknowing (Wright) | Diffusion Sampling (AI) |
|--------------------|-----------------------|
| The poet empties their mind of prior associations | The model starts from pure Gaussian noise — no signal |
| Prior knowledge is suspended, not eliminated | The noise is structured by the model's architecture, not random |
| The image gradually reveals itself through receptive looking | The image gradually emerges through iterative denoising |
| The poet allows the image to "guide" the looking experience | The text prompt conditions the denoising trajectory |
| Success = genuine surprise, discovery of what was not expected | Success = coherent image that was not pre-specified |
| Failure = projecting prior knowledge onto the image (seeing what you expect) | Failure = mode collapse (generating what's most probable, not most appropriate) |

Both describe a **generative process that begins from minimal information and progressively resolves into specificity**. Both require the agent (poet/model) to resist the temptation to jump to a conclusion too early. Both produce their best results when the resolution process is gradual, iterative, and responsive to incoming signals rather than pre-committed to an outcome.

### Bion's "Capacity to Forget" = Temperature Parameter

In AI generation, the **temperature parameter** controls how much the model deviates from the most probable output. Low temperature = deterministic, predictable, "knowing." High temperature = exploratory, surprising, "unknowing."

Wright's therapeutic recommendation — cultivate the capacity to forget, eschew desire and understanding — is the humanistic equivalent of **running the model at high temperature**: allowing the associative process to explore unexpected regions of the latent space rather than collapsing to the mode.
