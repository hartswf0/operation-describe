## Citation

Kulkarni, Chinmay, Stefania Druga, Minsuk Chang, Alex Fiannaca, Carrie Cai, and Michael Terry. "A Word is Worth a Thousand Pictures: Prompts as AI Design Material." *arXiv preprint arXiv:2303.12647* (2023).

## Core Argument

Through a within-subjects study with 14 non-professional designers, Kulkarni et al. find that text-to-image prompts function as **reflective design material** — not instructions, not specifications, but material you work *with*, not *through*.

### Key Claims

1. **Prompts as Material**: The paper's title inverts the cliché ("a picture is worth a thousand words") to argue that in the TTI regime, the *word* is the generative substrate. This is Bajohr's operative ekphrasis observed empirically.

2. **Rapid Exploration**: TTI models allow non-experts to explore a design space faster than traditional tools — but the exploration is *verbal* (prompt iteration), not visual (sketch iteration).

3. **Pair Design Dynamics**: In collaborative settings, prompts become **shared design artifacts** — objects that two designers negotiate, modify, and reflect upon together. The prompt is not just input; it is the *medium of collaboration*.

## The Non-Obvious Connection: Prompts as Oral Formulae

Kulkarni's finding that prompts are "reflective design material, facilitating exploration, iteration, and reflection" maps directly onto **Parry-Lord oral-formulaic theory**: Homer's epithets and formulas were also "reflective design material" — verbal building blocks that the oral poet iterated, combined, and reflected upon in live performance.

| Oral-Formulaic Composition | TTI Prompt Design |
|---------------------------|-------------------|
| Formula: "rosy-fingered dawn" | Prompt modifier: "cinematic lighting, golden hour" |
| Epithet: "swift-footed Achilles" | Style tag: "in the style of Studio Ghibli" |
| Theme: pre-composed narrative blocks | Template: prompt structures reused across sessions |
| Singer's competence = formula repertoire | Prompter's competence = modifier vocabulary |
| Audience shapes performance | Collaborator shapes prompt iteration |

Both show that **verbal creation in a generative system is inherently formulaic** — not in the pejorative sense (uncreative, repetitive), but in the Parry-Lord sense: formulae are the building blocks of real-time verbal creation within a responsive environment.
