## Citation

Clüver, Claus. "A new look at an old topic: ekphrasis revisited." *Todas as Letras* 19, no. 1 (2017): 30–44. DOI: 10.5935/1980-6914/letras.v19n1p30-44

## Core Argument

Clüver argues that Heffernan's dominant definition ("verbal representation of a visual representation") is **untenable** because it excludes:
- Non-representational painting and sculpture
- Architecture
- Abstract and conceptual art

He proposes replacing the "intermedial transposition" model with a definition centered on **the viewer's encounter**: ekphrasis "primarily verbalizes a viewer's encounter with (a) non-kinetic visual configuration(s)."

## Key Claims

### Against Heffernan
Heffernan's definition (1993, reiterated 2015) assumes that visual art must be *representational* to be ekphrastically described. This excludes Rothko, Pollock, Mondrian, and all architecture.

### Against Paragone
Kennedy's recent studies show "contemporary critical ekphrasis and the surrounding discourse have turned against the model of a paragone based on representation."

### The Encounter Model
Ekphrasis should be understood not as *transposition* between media but as the **verbalization of a viewer's encounter** with a visual configuration. This shifts the focus from the object to the subject — from "what does the art look like?" to "what happens when someone stands before it?"

## Position in the Paragone

Clüver sides with neither hope nor fear. He dissolves the paragone altogether by making ekphrasis about **encounter** rather than **competition**.
