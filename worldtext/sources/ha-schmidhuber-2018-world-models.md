## Citation

Ha, David, and Jürgen Schmidhuber. "World Models." *arXiv preprint arXiv:1803.10122* (2018). [Interactive version at worldmodels.github.io]

## Core Argument

Ha & Schmidhuber propose that an artificial agent can learn a compressed **spatial and temporal representation of its environment** — a "world model" — and use that internal model to simulate future outcomes, plan actions, and even **dream** within the model (train entirely inside hallucinated environments).

### Architecture (V-M-C)

The agent has three components:
1. **V (Vision Model)**: a variational autoencoder (VAE) that compresses high-dimensional visual observations into a compact latent vector **z**
2. **M (Memory Model)**: an RNN (MDN-RNN) that predicts future latent vectors — it learns **temporal dynamics** and can hallucinate plausible futures
3. **C (Controller)**: a simple linear model that maps z + hidden state → actions

### Key Insight: Dreaming in Latent Space
The agent can train its controller **entirely within the dream** generated by the M model, without ever interacting with the real environment. This is training inside a hallucinated world.

## Position in the Cosmos

This paper operates at the junction of **world-building** and **generative AI**. The "world model" concept is structural kin to the WorldText cosmos: both assert that a compressed, learnable representation of a complex environment can substitute for (and generate within) that environment. 

The VAE's latent space **z** is the AI equivalent of Krieger's "spatial form" — a frozen, compressed representation of a temporal experience. Bajohr's CLIP embedding space is a descendant of Ha & Schmidhuber's latent code.
