# Place: The Shield Surface

The inscribed surface of Achilles' shield — the ur-ekphrastic place. A concentric cosmography in bronze, tin, gold, and silver, forged by Hephaestus and described by Homer in Iliad XVIII.478–608.

## Topology

The shield is organized concentrically:
1. **Center**: Earth, sea, sky, sun, moon, constellations
2. **First ring**: Two cities — one at peace (wedding, marketplace, trial), one at war (siege, ambush, battle)
3. **Second ring**: Agricultural scenes — plowing, harvest, vineyard
4. **Third ring**: Pastoral — cattle, sheep
5. **Fourth ring**: Dance floor (χορός)
6. **Rim**: River Ocean encircling everything

## Significance

This is a **world inscribed on a surface** — the foundational structural pattern of all ekphrasis and, by extension, of all WorldText. The shield is simultaneously an artifact, a description, and a cosmos.
