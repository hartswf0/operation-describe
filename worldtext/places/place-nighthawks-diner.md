Edward Hopper's *Nighthawks* (1942) — one of the most ekphrastically proliferative images in American art. An all-night diner with four figures under fluorescent light, endlessly re-described by poets and critics. Bundschuh (2018) traces how ekphrastic responses to this painting have been reshaped by the digital age.
