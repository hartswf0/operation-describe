## Definition

The generation gate is the threshold between prompt and output — the moment when language becomes image (or text becomes generated text). Before the gate: human intention, accumulated context, deliberate word choice. After the gate: statistical sampling, latent traversal, probabilistic generation.

The critical property of the generation gate: it is **one-way opaque**. The operator can see what goes in (the prompt) and what comes out (the generated artifact). But the transformation that happens inside is black-boxed. The operator cannot trace the specific path from prompt to output. This is the anti-memex: the trail is hidden.

## What Crosses

- The prompt's text (visible)
- The prompt's implicit context (model's interpretation of genre, style, subject)
- The training distribution's statistical center of mass (invisible, dominant)
- The model's current parameter state (invisible, deterministic given seed)

## What Does Not Cross

- The operator's intention (the model does not know WHY the prompt was written)
- The operator's criteria for success (the model does not know what would satisfy)
- The operator's history with this subject matter (the model has no memory across sessions unless context is provided)
