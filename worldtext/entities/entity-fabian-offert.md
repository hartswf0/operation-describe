## Identity

Fabian Offert. Media theorist and AI researcher. Theorist of the **latent space as medium** — the claim that the latent space is not merely a technical substrate but a new kind of optical medium with its own properties, affordances, and politics.

## Key Contribution

**Latent space as medium**: Offert argues that the latent space should be understood not as an implementation detail but as a medium in its own right — analogous to paint, celluloid, or the printed page. Like any medium, it has:

- **Affordances**: what it makes possible (continuous interpolation, sampling, navigation)
- **Constraints**: what it makes difficult or impossible (precise control, guaranteed outputs)
- **Politics**: whose visual culture it encodes (training data demographics, platform biases)
- **Aesthetics**: what it tends to produce when left to its defaults ([[concept-platform-realism]])

This reframes operative ekphrasis: the prompt is not just a verbal instruction but an act performed *within* a medium. The medium shapes the outcome as much as the intent does — just as oil paint's material properties shape the painting.

## Position in the Cosmos

Offert provides the theoretical foundation for [[world-latent-space]] — the world page exists but lacks this medium-theoretic grounding. His claim connects to:

- Ellestrom's four modalities (the material modality of the latent space)

- Webb's phantasia (the ancient analogue: the mind's image-store as a medium of imagination)
