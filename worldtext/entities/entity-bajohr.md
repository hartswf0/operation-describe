Hannes Bajohr is a literary scholar and media theorist who coined the concept of **operative ekphrasis** — the collapse of the text/image distinction in multimodal AI.

## Key Contributions

- **"Operative ekphrasis: the collapse of the text/image distinction in multimodal AI"** (*Word & Image*, 2024) — The foundational text for [[world-operative-ekphrasis]]. Bajohr argues that:
  1. The digital realm must be divided into a **sequential** part (classic algorithms) and a **connectionist** part (artificial neural networks).
  2. Only in the connectionist part does the text/image distinction technically collapse.
  3. Multimodal AI systems (CLIP, DALL·E) encode text and image as the same type of information.
  4. This means ekphrasis in the context of multimodal AI "goes beyond the separation of or transition between text and image, but rather **transcends this difference**."

## Position in Cosmological Debates

Bajohr occupies the most radical position: not merely complicating the paragone (Mitchell) or expanding the definition (Clüver), but declaring the foundational distinction **technically annihilated** within the connectionist regime. This makes operative ekphrasis the **event horizon** of the cosmos.
