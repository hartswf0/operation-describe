Ruth Webb is a classicist who recovered the **ancient rhetorical** meaning of ekphrasis — *enargeia*, the art of making listeners "see" through words alone.

## Key Contributions

- **"Ekphrasis ancient and modern: The invention of a genre"** (1999) — Demonstrated that the modern literary definition (Heffernan) diverges sharply from the ancient rhetorical practice. Ancient ekphrasis was about **persuasion and vivid imagination**, not competition between arts.
- **Ekphrasis, Imagination and Persuasion in Ancient Rhetorical Theory and Practice** (2016) — Full study of ekphrasis in the progymnasmata tradition (2nd–6th centuries CE). Links ekphrasis to ancient theories of *phantasia* (imagination) and *pathos* (emotion).
