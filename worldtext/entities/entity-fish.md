## Identity

Stanley Fish (b. 1938). American literary theorist, legal scholar, and public intellectual. Author of *Is There a Text in This Class?* (1980). Key figure in reader-response criticism.

## Key Contribution to the Cosmos

### Interpretive Communities

Fish argues that textual meaning is not "in" the text (as formalism claims) or "in" the reader (as naive subjectivism claims) but in the **interpretive community** — the shared set of assumptions, conventions, and reading strategies that a community employs. Different communities produce different legitimate readings of the same text.

**Cosmos application**: Fish's interpretive communities are the **training distribution** of the human bio-inference engine. Just as a diffusion model's outputs are bounded by the statistical properties of its training data, a reader's interpretations are bounded by their community's interpretive norms.

This provides the human analogue of [[concept-platform-realism]]: the reader does not freely interpret any more than the prompter freely generates. Both navigate a pre-structured interpretive space. Fish's communities are Meyer's platform realism for humans.

| Fish | Meyer |
|------|-------|
| The interpretive community constrains the reader | The training distribution constrains the model |
| Different communities produce different readings | Different training runs produce different models |
| No "neutral" reading is possible | No "neutral" generation is possible |
| The community IS the meaning | The distribution IS the default aesthetic |
