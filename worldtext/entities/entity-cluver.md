Claus Clüver is a comparative literature scholar who expanded the concept of ekphrasis beyond verbal/visual to encompass all **intersemiotic transposition**.

## Key Contributions

- **"Ekphrasis Reconsidered" (1997)** — Argued that Heffernan's definition excludes non-representational art and architecture. Proposed a broader definition based on intersemiotic practice.
- **"Intermediality and Interarts Studies" (2007)** — Positioned ekphrasis as a subcategory of intermedial reference.
- **"A new look at an old topic" (2017)** — Updated critique. Proposed that ekphrasis "primarily verbalizes a viewer's encounter with (a) non-kinetic visual configuration(s)."
