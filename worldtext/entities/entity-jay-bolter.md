## Identity

Jay David Bolter. Media theorist. Co-author (with Richard Grusin) of *Remediation: Understanding New Media* (1999) — the foundational framework for understanding how new media refashion prior media. Co-author (with [[entity-watson-hartsoe]]) of "From Imagetext to Worldtext" — the central paper of the OPERATION DESCRIBE cosmos.

## Key Contributions

1. **Remediation** (with Grusin, 1999): The dual logic of immediacy (transparent erasure of the medium) and hypermediacy (foregrounding of the medium). This framework directly structures the [[concept-seamless-vs-exposed]] fork: the seamless path = immediacy; the exposed path = hypermediacy.

2. **[[concept-worldtext]]** (with Watson-Hartsoe, 2026): The extension of Mitchell's imagetext into interactive, spatio-temporal environments compiled from language. The operative word produces not a picture but a world.

3. **Thick prompting** (with Watson-Hartsoe, 2026): Deliberately recalling Geertz's thick description — iterative, context-rich prompt construction that describes not just a desired image but the world from which it is drawn. Mapped in the cosmos at [[thick-prompt]].

4. **Algorithmic remediation** (2023, in IMAGE): AI remediates not media forms but aesthetic vibes — nameable atmospheric patterns. Extended by [[entity-roland-meyer]] in the [[concept-platform-realism]] framework.

5. **The Homeric analogy**: The paper argues that Homer's Shield of Achilles is the benchmark for operative ekphrasis — 130 lines that specify not an image but an entire world. The shield is the first [[concept-worldtext]].

## Position in the Cosmos

Bolter is the bridge entity between the Galaxy of Intermedial Worlds and the Galaxy of Generative AI Worlds. His remediation framework was already the dominant theory of media transformation; the worldtext paper extends it from "how new media refashion old media" to "how AI compiles worlds from language."
