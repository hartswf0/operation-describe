## Identity

**Ian Cheng** (b. 1984) is an American artist working at the intersection of live simulation, artificial life, and worlding practice. His work treats worlds not as static representations but as **autonomous, evolving systems** whose dynamics continue beyond authorial control.

## Key Works

- **Emissaries** trilogy (2015–2017): Live simulations with behavioral drives, environmental conditions, and open-ended evolution. Shown at MoMA PS1, Serpentine Galleries, Fondazione Sandretto.
- **BOB (Bag of Beliefs)** (2018–2019): An AI creature with a personality, memory, and capacity to evolve through audience interaction.
- *Emissaries Guide to Worlding* (2018): Theoretical companion text. Proposes worlding as the creation of conditions for open-ended simulation, not the design of fixed narratives.

## Core Contribution

Cheng's practice provides the operational precedent for the **world watcher** concept:

```
<World Watcher> [does_not_reduce] <World> [to] <Single Event>
  [watches] <Persistent Unfolding>
  [watches] <Behavioral Drift>
  [watches] <Emergent Pattern>
  [watches] <How Rule + Environment + Agent Produce Surprise>
```

The world watcher is an audience position that refuses both passive spectatorship and authorial control. It attends to **worlding as ongoing process**.

## Relevance to Cosmos

Cheng's work directly grounds the distinction between [[concept-operative-observer|operative observation]] and participant observation:

- **Participant observer** joins a world
- **World watcher** attends to a world's unfolding
- **Operative observer** watches *while editing conditions*

His simulations are also the clearest existing example of **[[concept-synthetic-place|synthetic place engines]]**: systems that produce place-like conditions through inference, feedback, constraint, and interactive updating.
