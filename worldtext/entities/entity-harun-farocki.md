## Identity

Harun Farocki (1944–2014). Filmmaker, artist, writer. Theorist of **operative images** — images that steer rather than represent.

## Key Contributions

1. **Operative images**: Farocki identified a class of images that are not made for human viewing but for machine action — missile guidance imagery, surveillance feeds, production-line machine vision. These images do not represent the world; they *operate* on it.

2. **The genealogy of operativity**: Farocki's operative images connect Bajohr's "operative ekphrasis" to a deeper history. The "operative" in operative ekphrasis inherits from Farocki's "operative" in operative images — both describe the moment when a sign ceases to be representational and becomes *performative*, *causal*, *steering*.

3. **Cybernetic lineage**: Farocki's operative images are, behind everything, cybernetic instruments — they close the feedback loop between perception and action. Wiener's steersman operates through images that steer. The prompter is a steersman; the latent space is the ocean; the prompt is the rudder.

## The Open Question

Is the "operative" in operative ekphrasis the same kind of operativity as in Farocki's operative images — or only analogous?

- **Same**: Both describe signs that cause action rather than representing reality. The prompt is an operative sign in Farocki's sense.
- **Different**: Farocki's operative images are inhuman — they bypass human perception entirely. Operative ekphrasis still involves human intent (the prompter) and human evaluation (the viewer). The human is in the loop.
- **Synthesis**: Operative ekphrasis is the **humanization** of Farocki's operative image — a cybernetic sign that steers generation but remains within a human feedback loop. [[concept-reactive-ekphrasis]] (autonomous chains) is closer to Farocki's pure operativity — and the convergence it produces is the visual equivalent of the missile hitting its target: function fulfilled, meaning extinguished.
