Clifford Geertz (1926–2006) was an American anthropologist who founded **interpretive anthropology** and coined the methodological term **thick description**.

## Key Contributions

- **"Thick Description: Toward an Interpretive Theory of Culture"** (1973) — The opening chapter of *The Interpretation of Cultures*. Drawing on Gilbert Ryle's distinction between a twitch and a wink, Geertz argued that ethnography's task is not to record behavior but to interpret the layered meanings of cultural action.
- **"Deep Play: Notes on a Balinese Cockfight"** (1973) — The paradigmatic scene of thick description. The cockfight is read as a "text" encoding status, masculinity, economic risk, and Balinese social hierarchy.
- Culture as "webs of significance" — Geertz's core metaphor: humans are suspended in webs of meaning they themselves have spun, and cultural analysis is the interpretation of those webs.
