Homer (c. 8th century BCE) is the attributed author of the *Iliad* and *Odyssey* and, within this cosmos, **the first ekphrast** — the originator of extended visual description in Western literature.

## Cosmological Role

Homer's description of the Shield of Achilles (Iliad XVIII.478–608) is the **origin point** of the entire galaxy of ekphrastic worlds. The passage describes Hephaestus forging a shield inscribed with a miniature cosmos: earth, sea, sky, two cities (peace and war), agricultural scenes, a vineyard, cattle, a dance floor, and the encircling Ocean.

This is not merely the first ekphrasis — it is the first **world-on-a-surface**: a cosmos inscribed on an artifact. Every subsequent ekphrastic object echoes this originary act.
