## Identity

Wolfgang Iser (1926–2007). German literary theorist. Co-founder (with Hans Robert Jauss) of the Constance School of reception aesthetics. Author of *The Act of Reading* (1978) and *The Implied Reader* (1972).

## Key Contributions to the Cosmos

### 1. Gaps and Indeterminacy
Every literary text contains **gaps** — places of indeterminacy where the reader must supply connections, fill absences, and produce meaning not explicitly given. The gaps are not failures but constitutive features: they transform reading from passive reception to active production.

**Cosmos application**: In the [[concept-bio-inference]] thesis, Iser's gaps are isomorphic to Gaussian noise in a diffusion model. The reader "denoises" textual gaps using their accumulated experiential weights, just as the model denoises random noise using its training data. Without gaps, there is no reading. Without noise, there is no denoising. Indeterminacy is the engine of inference.

### 2. Concretization
Iser's term for the reader's act of gap-filling: the production of a determinate mental image or meaning from an indeterminate textual signal. Concretization is always partial, always perspectival, and always revisable.

**Cosmos application**: Concretization IS generation. The reader's concretization of a text = the model's generation from a prompt. Both are conditioned completions of sparse signals.

### 3. The Wandering Viewpoint
The reader's attention moves through the text sequentially, building and revising expectations at each step. The "wandering viewpoint" is Iser's term for the reader's dynamic, temporally structured engagement.

**Cosmos application**: The wandering viewpoint = the autoregressive decoder's token-by-token generation, or the diffusion model's step-by-step denoising. At each step, the state is revised in light of what has come before.
