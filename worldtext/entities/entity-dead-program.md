## Identity

The **Dead Program** is Peter Naur's term for a program (or world) that still runs but that nobody can modify coherently. It is the default state of every generative environment built without a Worldtext. The files remain — inert, unmodifiable, accumulating contradiction — until someone tries to patch them and the coherence collapses.

## Core Mechanism

A program becomes dead when:
1. The person(s) who possessed the theory leave
2. The theory is not transferred (taught) to a successor
3. New modifiers treat the codebase as residue — patching without understanding
4. Each patch introduces contradictions that compound
5. The system eventually becomes unmodifiable

Applied to worldtext:
1. The operator stops loading `prime-prompt.md` into sessions
2. The worldtext pages remain but become inert files
3. A new operator adds content without understanding the commitment structure
4. Contradictions accumulate silently
5. The worldtext becomes a wiki — residue, not theory

## Relationship to Cosmos

The Dead Program is the **terminus** the entire Worldtext apparatus is designed to prevent. Every ritual (INGEST, QUERY, LINT, AUDIT), every diagnostic program, every cross-link and commitment exists to keep the theory alive — to ensure that someone possesses the theory of the world, not merely the files.
