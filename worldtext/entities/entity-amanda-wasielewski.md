## Identity

Amanda Wasielewski. Art historian and media theorist. Author of "Unnatural Images" (*Critical Inquiry*, 2024) — a rigorous photography-theory analysis of AI-generated images that argues for continuity rather than rupture.

## Key Contributions

1. **Indexicality broken, appearance preserved**: AI images are "unnatural" in the computer-vision sense (synthesized, not captured from light) yet appear photographically "natural." The causal bond with reality ([[entity-krieger]]'s "natural sign") is absent — but the *aesthetic surface* of indexicality survives intact. The seamless path pursues an indexical image that the medium cannot deliver.

2. **Form over process**: Wasielewski's central argument — AI-generated photographs ARE photographs, not because of their process but because photographic *form* (the visual qualities associated with photography) is how they function culturally. "Photographic verisimilitude, not indexicality, is the root of the illusion."

3. **Baudrillard's hyperreal**: Wasielewski brings Baudrillard to bear: "generation by models of a real without origin or reality." AI images are the fulfillment of the hyperreal — images that precede their referents. Baudrillard's words "fit eerily well."

4. **AI glitches as modified punctum**: Where Barthes' punctum is the accidental, contingent detail in a photograph that "pricks" the viewer, AI glitches are a *statistical* punctum — contingency produced by the generation process rather than by the photographic encounter with reality. Statistical contingency, not material contingency.

5. **Galton/Sekula genealogy**: Rooted in Foucauldian power critique rather than aesthetics, Wasielewski gives the statistical composite argument a **surveillance and eugenics** dimension. The composite photograph was born in the service of racial classification and police identification. AI's statistical averaging inherits this genealogy.

6. **Farocki connection**: Wasielewski connects AI images to [[entity-harun-farocki]]'s operative images as the fulfillment of photography's objectification trajectory — images that act rather than represent.

## Position in the Cosmos

Wasielewski bridges the photorealism problem and the politics-of-the-threshold synthesis. Her argument that AI images are photographs *by form, not by process* is the strongest answer to the overview's question about the seamless path. The indexicality argument connects directly to the cosmos's Bridge 4 (Krieger's natural sign illusion = VAE encoding) but adds the critical insight that the illusion is now *technically sustained* without its original ground.
