Murray Krieger (1923–2000) was a literary theorist who argued ekphrasis is the **illusion of the natural sign** — language pretending to be a transparent window onto visual experience.

## Key Contributions

- **Ekphrasis: The Illusion of the Natural Sign** (1992) — Ekphrasis creates the illusion that words can function like images, bridging the gap between arbitrary linguistic signs and motivated visual signs. This illusion is productive, not deceptive.
