## Identity

Hito Steyerl. Artist, filmmaker, and writer. Author of "Mean Images" (*New Left Review*, 2023) — the sharpest critical formulation of what AI images are and what they reveal about visual culture.

## Key Contributions

1. **Mean images**: AI images are statistical means of the training corpus — mean in both senses (average and mediocre). The naive ekphrastic hope for a "faithful" image runs into the discovery that what AI produces is not any coherent tradition of photorealism but a statistical average across competing visual conventions: "**hallucinated mediocrity**."

2. **The Galton composite analogy**: Like Francis Galton's composite photographs — which averaged faces to produce a racial "type" — diffusion models produce images optimized for the center of the distribution, not for any particular vision. The "natural sign" ([[entity-krieger]]) turns out to be a statistical norm. This gives the statistical-composite argument a eugenic and surveillance genealogy.

3. **Likenesses replaced by likelihoods**: Steyerl's sharpest formulation. What AI produces is not a likeness (a representation of something) but a likelihood (a statistically probable configuration). The photographic index is replaced by the statistical index.

## Position in the Cosmos

Steyerl provides the **critical counterweight** to the cosmos's predominantly structural-formal analysis. Her argument that AI images are "mean" (mediocre) challenges the affirmative reading of [[concept-abundance]] and deepens [[concept-platform-realism]]. She is the voice that asks: what happens when the ekphrastic tradition's noblest aspiration — making the absent present through language — produces not visions but averages?
