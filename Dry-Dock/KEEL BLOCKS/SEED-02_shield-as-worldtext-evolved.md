## EVOLVED TEXT (447 words)

The decisive shift is not from image to text but from urn to shield. Brooks's *Well Wrought Urn* (1947) canonized the poem as a resolved, autonomous object whose meaning inheres in its finished verbal form. That critical revolution shaped how modern literary study imagines ekphrasis — through the figure of the completed artwork. The urn names the prestige of the resolved object. The shield names something older and now technically urgent: world-making as process.

In Book 18 of the *Iliad*, Homer does not list decorative motifs on a finished surface. The shield begins with cosmic order — earth, sea, sky, sun, moon, constellations — and unfolds two cities (one staging marriage, law, and festival; the other staging siege, ambush, and slaughter), fields under cultivation, harvest, vintage, herding, and dance, with Ocean encircling the rim. Taplin (1980) demonstrated that the shield occupies a position both within and beyond the poem's narrative: war is the plot; the shield is the counter-plot — the life Achilles will defend, destroy, and never fully inhabit. Mitchell (1994) argues that the shield "represents more of Homer's world than the epic proper" because it includes what the narrowed action of heroic war excludes: plowing, litigation, marriage, and everyday life. Moretti (1996) names this totalizing ambition directly: the shield is a "world text."

Lessing understood the formal mechanism with unusual precision: Homer shows not the shield as a static image but the divine craftsman bringing it into being through temporal succession. Webb (2009) confirms: ancient ekphrasis accommodated process no less than object. The ekphrastic center of gravity falls on temporal fabrication, not static display.

This structure maps directly onto Ha and Schmidhuber's (2018) V-M-C architecture for world models. The cosmos-to-Ocean boundary establishes the compressed latent space (Vision). The two cities model social state transitions — which action leads to which consequence (Memory). The agricultural cycle sustains temporal consistency across seasonal sequence (Memory). The surface textures — golden vines, silver fish, dancers in fine linen — are probabilistically generated from the underlying rule system, not the reverse (Vision). Hephaestus at the forge is the Controller: a compact policy that acts within the system's own representations.

Current world models exhibit this same structure. DeepMind's Genie 2 (Bruce et al. 2024) generates navigable, interactive environments from text prompts and simulates the consequences of action. World Labs' Marble produces persistent, editable 3D worlds. In the terminology of Garcez and Lamb (2023), both are neurosymbolic hybrids: probabilistic surfaces generated atop deterministic rule engines. The prompt functions not as sovereign command but as control signal (Wiener 1948) — one regulatory input collaborating with training data, safety filters, physics engines, and procedural rules.

An image has no consequences. A world does. Without consequence, worldtext is only scenery with ambition. And as Chun (2011) has argued for software generally: operation is never innocent.
