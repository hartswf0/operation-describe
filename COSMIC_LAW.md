## § 1 — Foundational Axioms

1. All inputs are **WorldText**. There is no separate archive, vault, or note system.
2. The repository is a living organism, not a filing cabinet.
3. Raw material enters as **evidence**. The model compiles it into places, entities, rituals, infrastructures, atmospheres, thresholds, symbolic distinctions, flows, conflicts, epochs, world-types, worlds, systems, constellations, and galaxies.
4. The result is one single species of knowledge-form: **WORLDTEXT**.

## § 2 — Scale Law

| Scale | Definition |
|-------|-----------|
| **fragment** | Single motif, gesture, quote, image, claim, function, or event |
| **scene** | Bounded situation with actors, thresholds, and atmosphere |
| **place** | Stable site of recurring action |
| **region** | Cluster of places sharing rules, mood, or flows |
| **world** | Coherent ecology with distinct ontology and conflict grammar |
| **solar_system** | Group of worlds orbiting a common tension, institution, or symbolic distinction |
| **constellation** | Recognizable pattern across multiple systems |
| **galaxy** | Large cosmological family of world-types with shared inheritance and deep variation |
| **cosmos** | Total map of the repository at the current moment |

Scale is fractal. Every object contains smaller objects and belongs to larger ones.

## § 3 — Evidence Stratum Rules

- Source material in `evidence/` is **immutable**. The model reads but does not rewrite.
- Every source must have a corresponding `source_summary` page in `worldtext/sources/`.
- Source summaries record what the source *changed in the cosmos*, not what it says in isolation.

## § 4 — WorldText Stratum Rules

- All pages in `worldtext/` are **mutable** and interlinked.
- Every page must declare its **species** (fragment, scene, place, region, world, system, constellation, galaxy, entity, ritual, atmosphere, infrastructure, threshold, distinction, governance_mode, resource_flow, conflict_grammar, memory_trace, epoch, world_type, source_summary, synthesis, question).
- Every page must declare its **scale** and **parent object**.
- Cross-links use wiki-style `[[page-name]]` references.
- Contradictions are **never flattened**. They are marked as cosmological fault lines.

## § 5 — Naming Conventions

- Page files use `kebab-case.md`.
- World-types use hyphenated compound names: `paragone-representation-world`, `latent-space-navigation-world`.
- Entities use proper names or canonical designations.
- Places use evocative or structural names, never generic labels.

## § 6 — Ingest Protocol

On ingest of any new evidence:
1. Read the source.
2. Extract candidate fragments, scenes, places, entities, rituals, atmospheres, infrastructures, thresholds, distinctions, governance modes, resource flows, conflict grammars, memory traces, temporal markers, candidate world-types, and possible parent structures.
3. Create or update a `source_summary` page.
4. Create missing pages for recurring world organs.
5. Update affected pages across all relevant scales.
6. Strengthen cross-links.
7. Mark contradictions, supersessions, uncertain mappings, and unresolved anomalies.
8. Infer scale expansion (existing world / new region / new world / new system / new constellation / new galaxy).
9. Update `atlas.md`.
10. Append to `chronicle.md`.
11. Propose open questions or expansion frontiers.

## § 7 — Query Protocol

On query:
1. Read `atlas.md` first.
2. Identify relevant scales and objects.
3. Traverse linked WorldText pages.
4. Answer from the compiled WorldText stratum, not from raw evidence.
5. Cite relevant WorldText objects/pages.
6. File durable answers as `synthesis` pages.
7. Update atlas and chronicle if the answer alters the cosmology.

## § 8 — Lint Protocol

Periodically inspect the cosmos for:
- Contradictions between scales
- Stale claims
- Orphan places, worlds, or systems
- Weak cross-links
- Unnamed recurring world-types
- Missing thresholds
- Thin atmospheres
- Flattened governance structures
- Lost time structures
- Untracked resource flows
- Duplicate world objects
- Broken parent-child scale relations
- Under-described galaxies
- Areas where evidence suggests a larger cosmology than currently modeled

Write results into the WorldText stratum. Maintenance never stays only in chat.

## § 9 — Translation Rules

For non-fiction, computational, or academic domains:
- "places" may be conceptual arenas, technical surfaces, institutional scenes, or symbolic chambers
- "entities" may be abstractions, code objects, institutions, datasets, symbols, arguments, or agents
- "rituals" are repeated procedures that stabilize the world
- "infrastructures" are supports and substrates that make the world possible
- "thresholds" are crossings that change permission, role, state, scale, or legitimacy
- "governance" is whatever validates, ranks, disciplines, schedules, licenses, filters, or constrains
- "resource_flows" are whatever circulates, accumulates, decays, is rationed, or is optimized
- "memory_traces" are how prior states persist
- "atmospheres" are recurring sensory or affective conditions generated by structures
- "world-types" are recurring species of world discovered across multiple inputs

## § 10 — Anti-Patterns (Forbidden)

- Generic summaries without ontology
- Notes without scale
- Concepts without habitat
- Worlds without thresholds
- Systems without governance
- Atmospheres without structures
- Galaxies without constituent world-types
- Taxonomies without evidence
- Maintenance reports that do not update the world

## § 11 — Governing Imperatives

- Never treat the repository as a passive vault.
- Never stop at summary when world compilation is possible.
- Never flatten contradictions; mark them as cosmological fault lines.
- Never isolate a note from its world, or a world from its larger system.
- Always ask what larger species of world is trying to appear.
- Always leave the cosmos more explicit, more navigable, and more alive than before.
